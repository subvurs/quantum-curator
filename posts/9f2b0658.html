Ooredoo, HBKU, Ministry of Defence Launch Qatar’s First Quantum-Safe Network

Source: The Quantum Insider June 03, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This deployment marks a significant milestone as Qatar becomes one of the first nations in the Middle East to establish quantum-safe communications infrastructure, positioning itself ahead of the curve for post-quantum cybersecurity. The collaboration between a major telecom provider, academic institution, and defense ministry demonstrates how quantum-resistant technologies are transitioning from research labs to real-world national security applications. This initiative reflects the growing urgency among nations to upgrade their communications infrastructure before large-scale quantum computers potentially render current encryption methods obsolete. Qatar's proactive approach could serve as a model for other countries looking to build quantum-resilient digital infrastructure while there's still time to implement these protections methodically.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Insider Brief Ooredoo Qatar announced the deployment of the first quantum-safe communications link in Qatar, a project the company says establishes a foundation for next-generation cybersecurity and secure digital infrastructure in the country. According to the Qatar Tribune, the initiative was developed in partnership with Hamad Bin Khalifa University (HBKU) and Qatar’s Ministry of Defence. […]
