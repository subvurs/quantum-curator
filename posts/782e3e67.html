SeQure Quantum: The First Quantum-Safe Link in Critical Electrical Infrastructure in Chile

Source: The Quantum Insider May 25, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This deployment marks a significant milestone as one of the first quantum-safe implementations in critical electrical infrastructure, demonstrating that the quantum threat to encryption is moving from theoretical concern to practical preparation. Chile's proactive approach to securing power grid communications with quantum-resistant cryptography sets an important precedent for other nations, as electrical infrastructure represents one of the most vulnerable and essential targets for future quantum-enabled cyberattacks. The timing is particularly noteworthy given recent advances in quantum computing hardware that are steadily reducing the timeline for when large-scale quantum computers could break current encryption standards. This real-world implementation provides valuable data on how quantum-safe technologies perform in mission-critical environments, offering insights that will be crucial as governments and utilities worldwide begin their own quantum-readiness transitions.

— Mark Eatherly
