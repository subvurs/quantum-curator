Hybrid Clifford Codes via Operator Algebra Quantum Error Correction and Projective Representation Theory

Source: arXiv Quantum Physics June 01, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This work represents a significant theoretical advance in quantum error correction by extending Clifford codes—already a powerful generalization of stabilizer codes—into new hybrid territories that can simultaneously protect both classical and quantum information. The authors' use of operator algebra quantum error correction and projective representation theory opens up previously unexplored code families, potentially offering new pathways to more efficient error correction schemes that could handle mixed classical-quantum computational tasks. While highly theoretical, this mathematical framework could prove crucial for future quantum systems that need to process both types of information simultaneously, such as quantum-classical hybrid algorithms or quantum networking protocols. The extension beyond traditional stabilizer codes is particularly noteworthy, as it suggests these new hybrid codes might offer protection capabilities that conventional approaches cannot achieve.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Clifford codes are a natural generalization of quantum stabilizer codes based primarily on representation theory. This class of codes has previously been extended to the setting of quantum subsystem codes. We formulate a two-fold generalization of Clifford codes, for both the hybrid classical and quantum information and projective representation theory settings. This leads to new classes of hybrid subspace and subsystem Clifford codes. We extend the fundamental representation theoretic quantum error correction theorem to include these codes, based on the operator algebra quantum error correction framework. We also discuss several examples throughout the presentation, of both stabilizer and non-stabilizer type.
