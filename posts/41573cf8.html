La Sierra University Launches Quantum-Secure Pilot to Protect Data

Source: The Quantum Insider May 26, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This pilot program at La Sierra University represents an important milestone in the practical deployment of quantum-safe cryptography, as organizations increasingly recognize the urgent need to prepare for the quantum threat to current encryption methods. While quantum computers capable of breaking widely-used RSA and elliptic curve cryptography are still years away, the transition to post-quantum cryptographic standards requires extensive testing and implementation time, making early adoption efforts like this crucial for understanding real-world challenges. The collaboration with enQase demonstrates how specialized quantum security companies are partnering with educational institutions to accelerate the deployment of quantum-resistant solutions before they become absolutely necessary. This proactive approach by a mid-sized university could serve as a valuable case study for other institutions planning their own quantum-safe transitions.

— Mark Eatherly
