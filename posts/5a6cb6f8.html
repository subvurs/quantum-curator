Field Demonstration of Trusted-Node QKD over Deployed Single-Mode and Multi-Core Fiber Infrastructure

Source: Quantum Computing Report June 09, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This field demonstration represents a crucial step toward making quantum key distribution commercially viable by proving it can operate within real-world telecommunications infrastructure rather than controlled laboratory conditions. The collaboration between multiple Swedish universities showcases how trusted-node QKD can be integrated into existing fiber networks, potentially paving the way for quantum-secured communications at scale. What makes this particularly significant is the use of both single-mode and multi-core fiber infrastructure, demonstrating the flexibility needed for practical deployment across diverse network architectures. This work bridges the gap between quantum cryptography research and the telecommunications industry's need for quantum-safe security solutions in an era of advancing quantum computing threats.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

A collaborative research team representing Linköping University, KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Stockholm University, and Chalmers University of Technology has reported the physical deployment of a long-haul, trusted-node Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) link integrated into a dynamically reconfigurable telecommunications network.
