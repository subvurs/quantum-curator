Single metasurface could generate record numbers of trapped neutral atoms

Source: Physics World - Quantum March 16, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This breakthrough in metasurface technology represents a crucial step toward truly scalable neutral atom quantum computers, potentially enabling systems with over 100,000 qubits - a scale that could finally deliver practical quantum advantage for real-world problems. Neutral atoms have emerged as one of the most promising qubit platforms because they're naturally identical and can be arranged in flexible geometries, but previous trapping methods have struggled with the massive scalability needed for fault-tolerant quantum computing. By using a single metasurface to generate record numbers of trapped atoms, researchers are addressing one of the key engineering bottlenecks that has limited neutral atom systems to smaller qubit counts. This advance could accelerate the timeline for building quantum computers capable of tackling complex optimization problems, drug discovery, and cryptographic applications that require hundreds of thousands of high-fidelity qubits.

— Mark Eatherly
