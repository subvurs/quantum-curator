Johns Hopkins Team Models Quantum Noise on Superconducting Processors

Source: The Quantum Insider June 10, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This Johns Hopkins research tackles one of quantum computing's most fundamental challenges: accurately predicting and modeling the noise that corrupts quantum calculations in real-world processors. A sevenfold improvement in predictive accuracy represents a significant leap forward that could dramatically enhance how quantum systems are designed, calibrated, and operated. Better noise modeling directly translates to more reliable quantum computations and could accelerate the development of error correction schemes, bringing us closer to practical quantum advantage. This work is particularly valuable because it focuses on superconducting processors, the same technology used by major players like IBM and Google in their quantum systems.

— Mark Eatherly
