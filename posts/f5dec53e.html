Quantum computer generates strings of certifiably random numbers

Source: Physics World - Quantum April 10, 2025 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This breakthrough demonstrates one of quantum computing's most practically valuable near-term applications: generating truly random numbers that can be mathematically certified as unpredictable, unlike the pseudo-random numbers produced by classical computers. Quantinuum's achievement using trapped-ion technology showcases how quantum systems can leverage fundamental quantum mechanical properties like superposition and measurement uncertainty to create genuine randomness, which is crucial for cryptography, secure communications, and scientific simulations. The fact that this work also extends to studying quantum magnetism and knot theory highlights how today's quantum computers are becoming versatile research tools capable of tackling multiple complex problems simultaneously. This represents a significant step toward practical quantum advantage in applications where certified randomness provides clear benefits over classical alternatives.

— Mark Eatherly
