Logical qubits with erasure conversion using metastable neutral atoms

Source: Nature Physics June 12, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This breakthrough represents a significant step toward practical quantum error correction by demonstrating that metastable ytterbium-171 atoms can convert regular quantum errors into detectable "erasure" errors, which are much easier to correct than unknown errors. The ability to pinpoint exactly where errors occur, rather than having to guess their location, dramatically reduces the overhead required for quantum error correction protocols. This erasure conversion technique could substantially lower the threshold for achieving fault-tolerant quantum computing, bringing us closer to quantum computers that can run useful algorithms for extended periods without being overwhelmed by noise. The work builds on growing momentum in the field around erasure qubits, which many researchers now view as one of the most promising paths to scalable quantum computing.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Nature Physics, Published online: 12 June 2026; doi:10.1038/s41567-026-03309-0 Qubits with detectable erasure errors can support more efficient error correction than qubits with unlocated errors. Improved logical qubit performance using erasure conversion has now been demonstrated using ytterbium-171 atoms.
