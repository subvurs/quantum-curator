Quanscient and Haiqu Demonstrate Algorithm for Scalable Computational Nonlinear Fluid Simulations

Source: Quantum Computing Report April 02, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This collaboration between Quanscient and Haiqu represents a significant milestone in applying quantum computing to real-world engineering problems, successfully demonstrating the most complex quantum fluid dynamics simulation to date on IBM's Heron processor. The 15-step nonlinear fluid benchmark with obstacle interactions showcases how Quantum Lattice Boltzmann Methods could eventually revolutionize computational fluid dynamics, a critical tool used across industries from aerospace design to climate modeling. While current quantum hardware limitations mean this won't replace classical CFD simulations anytime soon, the work provides crucial proof-of-concept evidence that quantum algorithms can handle the mathematical complexity of fluid turbulence and nonlinear interactions. This achievement opens the door for future quantum advantage in solving some of the most computationally demanding problems in engineering and physics.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Quanscient and Haiqu have announced a new quantum algorithm designed to accelerate Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulations. The researchers successfully executed a 15-step nonlinear fluid benchmark involving an obstacle, which currently stands as the most physically complex hardware demonstration of a Quantum Lattice Boltzmann Method (QLBM).
