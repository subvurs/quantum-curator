Pasqal and True Nexus Partner to Optimize Alternative Protein Design via Quantum Computing

Source: Quantum Computing Report April 10, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This partnership represents a fascinating application of quantum computing to one of the most practical challenges facing the alternative protein industry - predicting how engineered proteins will actually behave in real food products. Protein folding and interaction modeling is notoriously computationally intensive, making it a natural fit for quantum advantage, particularly with Pasqal's neutral-atom approach that excels at simulating complex molecular systems. The collaboration highlights quantum computing's growing maturity as companies move beyond proof-of-concept demonstrations to tackle specific industrial problems that could have genuine economic impact. If successful, this could accelerate the development of plant-based and cultivated meat products by dramatically reducing the trial-and-error cycle currently required to achieve desired textures and functionality.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Pasqal and Saudi-based computational intelligence firm True Nexus (a branch of AI Bobby) have entered a strategic partnership to apply neutral-atom quantum computing to the study of protein functionality. The initiative focuses on addressing technical barriers in the alternative protein industry, specifically the difficulty of predicting how proteins behave—including gelation and texture—within complex food systems.
