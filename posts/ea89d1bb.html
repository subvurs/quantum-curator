Poland’s Poznan University of Technology Unveils IQM Quantum Computer to Drive Research And Education

Source: The Quantum Insider May 26, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This announcement marks a significant step in democratizing quantum computing access across Europe, as Poznan University of Technology becomes one of the first Polish institutions to host a dedicated quantum system on campus. The IQM Radiance R1 installation is particularly noteworthy because it provides students and researchers with hands-on experience using superconducting quantum processors, which is crucial for developing the next generation of quantum talent in Eastern Europe. This deployment aligns with Poland's national quantum strategy and represents the growing trend of universities investing in on-site quantum hardware rather than relying solely on cloud access, giving researchers more flexibility for experimental work and algorithm development. The move also strengthens IQM's position as a key European quantum hardware provider, competing with IBM and other players in the academic quantum computing market.

— Mark Eatherly
