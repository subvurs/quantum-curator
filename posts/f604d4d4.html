Quantum Fourier transform reaches 52 qubits, shattering the previous 27-qubit record

Source: Phys.org Quantum April 16, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This quantum Fourier transform breakthrough by ParityQC nearly doubles the previous record, pushing this foundational algorithm into the regime where it could soon tackle real-world problems. The quantum Fourier transform serves as the computational backbone for Shor's algorithm (threatening current encryption standards) and various quantum simulations in chemistry and finance, making this scaling milestone particularly significant. Running successfully on 52 qubits suggests that IBM's quantum hardware is reaching the threshold where quantum advantage might emerge for certain applications. This achievement represents a crucial step toward practical quantum computing, as the quantum Fourier transform is often the computational bottleneck that determines whether quantum algorithms can outperform their classical counterparts.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

The spin-off company ParityQC has implemented the largest quantum Fourier transform ever reported using an IBM quantum computer, thereby setting a new milestone on the path toward the industrial application of quantum computers. The quantum Fourier transform is a cornerstone algorithm with applications in cryptography, financial modeling, and materials science.
