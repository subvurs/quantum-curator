Quobly Secures €115 Million Series A to Bring Silicon-Based Quantum Computers to Market

Source: The Quantum Insider June 03, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This €115 million Series A for Quobly represents one of the largest quantum computing funding rounds in Europe and signals growing investor confidence in silicon-based quantum approaches as a viable alternative to superconducting and trapped-ion systems. The French company's focus on silicon quantum dots is particularly noteworthy because this technology could potentially leverage existing semiconductor manufacturing infrastructure, offering a path to more scalable and cost-effective quantum computers. Quobly's ambitious 2026 timeline for commercial deployment puts them in direct competition with established players like IBM and Google, while their silicon approach could provide advantages in terms of operating temperature and manufacturing compatibility. The substantial funding reflects the quantum industry's maturation, as investors increasingly back companies with clear commercialization roadmaps rather than pure research plays.

— Mark Eatherly
