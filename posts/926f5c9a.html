Horizon Quantum Expands Hardware Testbed with 256-Qubit Trapped-Ion System Placement in Ireland

Source: Quantum Computing Report June 12, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This expansion marks a significant step in Europe's quantum computing infrastructure development, as Horizon Quantum brings IonQ's advanced 256-qubit trapped-ion technology to Dublin through Ireland's ambitious Silicon Island strategy. The deployment represents more than just hardware placement - it signals Europe's growing commitment to establishing regional quantum capabilities rather than relying solely on systems housed in the US or other markets. With Ireland positioning itself as a semiconductor hub, this testbed could serve as a crucial access point for European researchers and companies looking to experiment with high-fidelity trapped-ion quantum computing. The partnership between Horizon's software infrastructure expertise and IonQ's proven hardware platform suggests a focus on making quantum computing more accessible to the broader European quantum ecosystem.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Integrated software infrastructure pioneer Horizon Quantum Holdings Ltd. has announced plans to deploy its second hardware testbed location at its European headquarters in Dublin, Ireland. The facility will host a sixth-generation, chip-based 256-qubit trapped-ion system developed by IonQ.
