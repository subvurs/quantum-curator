QuSecure Collaborating with NIST’s National Cybersecurity Center of Excellence to Address Post-Quantum Algorithm Migration

Source: The Quantum Insider March 31, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This collaboration between QuSecure and NIST's National Cybersecurity Center of Excellence represents a crucial step in preparing for the post-quantum cryptography transition that organizations worldwide must navigate as quantum computers threaten current encryption methods. The partnership highlights how the cybersecurity industry is moving beyond theoretical discussions about quantum threats to practical implementation challenges, focusing on developing real-world migration strategies that organizations can actually execute. This work is particularly timely given that NIST finalized its post-quantum cryptographic standards in 2024, and many organizations are now grappling with the complex task of upgrading their security infrastructure before quantum computers become capable enough to break existing encryption. The emphasis on "cryptographic agility" suggests they're working on solutions that will make future algorithm transitions smoother, learning from the current migration challenges to build more adaptable security systems.

— Mark Eatherly
