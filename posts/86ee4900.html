Amazon Braket launches trapped-ion quantum computer from Alpine Quantum Technologies

Source: AWS Quantum Computing Blog November 18, 2025 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article marks a significant expansion of Amazon Braket's quantum hardware ecosystem, introducing Alpine Quantum Technologies' IBEX Q1 trapped-ion system as the platform's newest 12-qubit offering. The addition is particularly noteworthy because trapped-ion systems typically offer superior qubit coherence and gate fidelities compared to superconducting alternatives, potentially providing Braket users with access to higher-quality quantum operations for algorithm development and testing. This move reinforces Amazon's strategy of creating a hardware-agnostic quantum cloud platform, giving researchers and developers the ability to benchmark algorithms across different quantum computing architectures—from IBM's superconducting systems to IonQ's trapped ions and now AQT's European-built hardware. For the broader quantum ecosystem, this represents another step toward making diverse quantum hardware accessible to a global developer community without requiring individual partnerships with each hardware provider.

— Mark Eatherly
