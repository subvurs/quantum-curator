High-fidelity entanglement and coherent multi-qubit mapping in an atom array

Source: Nature Physics June 12, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This Nature Physics study represents a significant step toward more versatile quantum computing architectures by demonstrating coherent transfer of entangled states between different types of qubits within the same ytterbium-171 atom system. The ability to map quantum information between different qubit encodings within atoms opens up powerful hybrid approaches where researchers can leverage the unique advantages of each qubit type - for example, using one encoding for long-term storage and another for fast gate operations. This coherent multi-qubit mapping capability could be particularly valuable for building more robust quantum processors that can dynamically optimize how they store and manipulate quantum information based on the specific computational task at hand. The work with ytterbium-171 atoms is especially noteworthy given this isotope's exceptional properties for quantum computing, including its long coherence times and precise control capabilities.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Nature Physics, Published online: 12 June 2026; doi:10.1038/s41567-026-03258-8 Ytterbium-171 atoms can store quantum information in different ways, with their own applications and advantages. Now the ability to coherently transfer entangled states between these different qubit types has been demonstrated.
