Atom Computing and Phasecraft Partner on Material Science Algorithms

Source: Quantum Computing Report June 15, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This partnership bridges a critical gap in quantum computing by bringing together hardware and software specialists to tackle real-world materials science challenges. Phasecraft's track record in developing efficient quantum algorithms specifically optimized for near-term hardware could help Atom Computing's neutral-atom systems reach practical utility faster than either company could achieve alone. The focus on clean energy applications like battery and solar materials is particularly strategic, as these problems involve molecular simulations that quantum computers are theoretically well-suited to solve, potentially accelerating the timeline to commercial quantum advantage. This collaboration exemplifies the industry's shift from pure hardware development toward integrated hardware-software stacks targeting specific, high-value applications.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Neutral-atom quantum hardware developer Atom Computing and quantum software specialist Phasecraft have signed a Memorandum of Understanding to collaborate on application-focused algorithms. The partnership aims to adapt Phasecraft’s hardware-optimized software to Atom’s neutral-atom architecture, with a primary focus on accelerating materials discovery for clean energy applications like advanced batteries and solar technology.
