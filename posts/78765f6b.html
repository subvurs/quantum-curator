Podcast with Scott Aaronson, Professor of Computer Science, UT Austin

Source: The Quantum Insider May 09, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This podcast offers valuable insights from one of quantum computing's most respected theorists, as Scott Aaronson is renowned for his balanced perspective on both the promise and limitations of quantum technologies. His discussion of the "narrowing case for quantum skepticism" is particularly noteworthy, suggesting that recent hardware advances are addressing many of the fundamental concerns that critics have raised about quantum computing's viability. Aaronson's expertise in quantum algorithms and his thoughtful approach to addressing commercialization hype makes this conversation essential listening for anyone seeking a grounded assessment of where the field truly stands today. The timing is especially relevant as the industry grapples with managing expectations while pursuing the challenging path toward fault-tolerant quantum systems.

— Mark Eatherly
