Rigetti Computing Announces $100 Million UK Investment for 1,000-Qubit Milestone

Source: Quantum Computing Report March 26, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This announcement signals Rigetti's ambitious push toward fault-tolerant quantum computing, as crossing the 1,000-qubit threshold represents a critical milestone where quantum systems may begin demonstrating practical advantages over classical computers for certain applications. The UK expansion reflects the growing global competition for quantum supremacy, with Rigetti joining IBM, Google, and other major players in the race to build large-scale quantum processors. While raw qubit count alone doesn't guarantee computational advantage due to factors like error rates and connectivity, Rigetti's multi-year timeline suggests they're taking a measured approach to scaling up their superconducting quantum systems. This investment also highlights the UK's emergence as a major quantum hub, following significant government commitments and the presence of established players like Oxford Quantum Computing.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Rigetti Computing (Nasdaq: RGTI) has committed up to $100 million to expand its operations in the United Kingdom, marking the company’s first major capital investment outside the United States. The primary objective is to deploy a quantum computing system exceeding 1,000 qubits within the next three to four years.
