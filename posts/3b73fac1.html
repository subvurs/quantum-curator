Researchers from Cleveland Clinic and IBM Simulate Protein Structures with Quantum Computing

Source: The Quantum Insider March 27, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This marks a significant milestone as the first quantum simulation of a complete protein's electronic structure, moving quantum computing beyond small molecules into the realm of actual biological systems. The 303-atom Trp-cage protein represents a substantial leap in complexity from previous quantum chemistry demonstrations, showcasing how hybrid quantum-classical workflows can tackle computationally intensive biological problems that are crucial for drug discovery and protein engineering. While still in early stages, this collaboration between Cleveland Clinic and IBM demonstrates quantum computing's potential to eventually revolutionize how we understand and manipulate biological systems at the molecular level. The use of IBM's Quantum Heron processor in a supercomputing workflow also highlights the emerging paradigm of quantum-centric computing, where quantum and classical resources work together to solve problems neither could handle alone.

— Mark Eatherly

Insider Brief A joint Cleveland Clinic-IBM research team has used quantum computing to demonstrate a hybrid quantum-classical workflow to approximate the electronic structure of a protein for the first time, IBM announced.
