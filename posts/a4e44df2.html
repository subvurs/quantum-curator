IQM to Deliver 54-Qubit System to Poland’s Galaxy Systemy Informatyczne

Source: Quantum Computing Report April 07, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This commercial agreement represents a significant milestone for both IQM's expansion into Eastern European markets and Poland's growing quantum computing ambitions. The 54-qubit IQM Radiance system being delivered to Galaxy Systemy Informatyczne in 2026 demonstrates how quantum hardware companies are increasingly targeting enterprise customers with on-premises solutions rather than just cloud-based access. The integration with existing HPC infrastructure is particularly noteworthy, as it reflects the emerging hybrid computing model where quantum processors work alongside classical supercomputers to tackle specific problem sets. This deal also highlights Poland's strategic investment in quantum technologies as the country positions itself as a regional hub for advanced computing capabilities.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

IQM Quantum Computers has entered into a commercial agreement with Galaxy Systemy Informatyczne for the on-premises delivery of an IQM Radiance 54-qubit system. Scheduled for installation at Galaxy’s data center in Zielona Góra, Poland, in the fourth quarter of 2026, the superconducting quantum computer will be integrated with the firm’s existing High-Performance Computing (HPC) infrastructure.
