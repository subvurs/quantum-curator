BW Digital and NUS to Advance Quantum-Ready Data Centres for Southeast Asia

Source: The Quantum Insider June 11, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This collaboration represents a crucial step toward making quantum computing infrastructure viable in Southeast Asia's challenging tropical climate, where traditional data centers already struggle with heat and humidity management. The partnership between BW Digital and NUS addresses a often-overlooked but critical aspect of quantum deployment: the specialized cooling, power, and environmental controls that quantum computers require to operate reliably outside of laboratory settings. Southeast Asia's rapid digital transformation and strategic location make it a key battleground for quantum infrastructure development, and this research could establish engineering standards that enable practical quantum computing across one of the world's most economically dynamic regions. The focus on "quantum-ready" frameworks suggests they're preparing for the inevitable transition when quantum systems become commercially viable, positioning the region ahead of the curve rather than scrambling to catch up.

— Mark Eatherly
