Superconducting innovation: SQMS shapes up for scalable success in quantum computing

Source: Physics World - Quantum June 05, 2025 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article highlights Fermilab's SQMS Center's ambitious push toward qudit-based quantum computing, which could represent a significant leap beyond today's qubit systems by utilizing quantum states with more than two levels per unit. The focus on qudits is particularly intriguing because these higher-dimensional quantum states could dramatically increase computational density and potentially simplify certain quantum algorithms, though they come with added complexity in control and readout. What makes this especially noteworthy is SQMS's emphasis on inherent scalability from the ground up, addressing one of quantum computing's most persistent challenges by designing systems that can grow without hitting fundamental roadblocks. This research direction could prove crucial as the field moves toward fault-tolerant quantum computers that require thousands or millions of quantum units working together.

— Mark Eatherly
