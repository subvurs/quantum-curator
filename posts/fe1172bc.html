Origin Quantum Launches Wukong-180 Quantum System

Source: The Quantum Insider May 15, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

Origin Quantum's launch of the Wukong-180 represents a significant milestone for China's quantum computing ambitions, showcasing the country's growing capabilities in developing fully autonomous quantum systems from chip to cloud. The emphasis on "full-industry-chain autonomy" signals China's strategic push to reduce dependence on foreign quantum technologies, particularly important given recent geopolitical tensions around quantum exports. What makes this announcement particularly noteworthy is that the system is immediately accepting global computing tasks, positioning Origin Quantum to compete directly with established players like IBM and Google in the quantum cloud services market. This development underscores the increasingly competitive landscape in superconducting quantum computing, where national champions are emerging to challenge Western dominance in this critical technology sector.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Origin Quantum unveiled its fourth-generation superconducting quantum computer, Origin Wukong-180, on Saturday. The quantum computer is equipped with a self-developed superconducting quantum chip and began accepting quantum computing tasks from across the globe starting Saturday, according to the company’s statement.
