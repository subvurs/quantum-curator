Schrödinger cat states like it hot

Source: Physics World - Quantum April 15, 2025 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This breakthrough demonstrates that quantum superposition states can survive at surprisingly high temperatures of 1.8 Kelvin, which is remarkably warm by quantum standards where most experiments require near absolute zero conditions. The ability to maintain these delicate "Schrödinger cat" states at higher temperatures could dramatically reduce the cooling requirements for quantum computers, potentially making them more practical and less expensive to operate. This finding challenges conventional wisdom about the fragility of quantum states and opens new possibilities for quantum devices that don't require extreme refrigeration, bringing us closer to room-temperature quantum computing applications. The research represents a significant step toward making quantum technology more accessible by relaxing one of its most demanding technical requirements.

— Mark Eatherly
