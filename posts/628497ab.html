QuEra Outlines 2028 Roadmap for 256-Logical-Qubit “Libra” System and Expanded AWS Cloud Partnership

Source: Quantum Computing Report June 15, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

QuEra's ambitious 2028 roadmap marks a significant milestone in the race toward practical quantum computing, with their Libra system targeting 256 logical qubits—a scale where genuinely useful quantum algorithms could begin to outperform classical computers on real-world problems. The partnership with AWS is particularly noteworthy because it signals growing confidence from major cloud providers that fault-tolerant quantum computing is transitioning from theoretical possibility to concrete engineering challenge. The "megaquop" classification (referring to quantum operations per second) suggests QuEra expects not just stable logical qubits, but the high-speed gate operations necessary for complex computations. While 2028 remains four years away and quantum hardware timelines have historically slipped, the specificity of this roadmap and AWS backing suggest neutral atom platforms are maturing into serious contenders alongside superconducting and trapped-ion approaches.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Neutral-atom hardware developer QuEra Computing and Amazon Web Services (AWS) have announced an expanded, multi-year strategic collaboration to bring the first fault-tolerant quantum computer to the cloud.
