Resource Estimates for Quantum Discrete Logarithm Computations on 256-bit Elliptic Curves

Source: Quantum Computing Report March 26, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This research represents a significant step toward making quantum cryptanalysis more practical by nearly halving the logical qubit requirements for breaking 256-bit elliptic curve cryptography, from over 2,000 qubits down to just 1,098. The finding brings the quantum threat to current encryption standards closer to reality, as major quantum computing companies are already targeting logical qubit counts in the hundreds to low thousands within the next decade. While we're still years away from fault-tolerant quantum computers with sufficient logical qubits, this optimization work helps cryptographers better understand the timeline for when organizations will need to migrate to post-quantum encryption standards. The research also demonstrates how algorithmic improvements can be just as important as hardware advances in determining when quantum advantage becomes practically achievable.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

A technical paper by Clémence Chevignard, Pierre-Alain Fouque, and André Schrottenloher, scheduled for EUROCRYPT 2026, describes a method to solve the Discrete Logarithm problem on 256-bit elliptic curves (ECDLP) using 1,098 logical qubits. This is a reduction from previous estimates of 2,124 logical qubits for the same security level.
