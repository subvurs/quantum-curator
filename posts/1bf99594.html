Curator's Take

This article captures a pivotal moment in cybersecurity as the quantum-safe cryptography landscape has dramatically matured from a niche concern to an industry-wide imperative. With NIST's post-quantum cryptography standards now finalized and the recent addition of HQC (Hamming Quasi-Cyclic) as a standardized algorithm in 2025, organizations are no longer asking "if" they need quantum-resistant security but "how fast" they can implement it. The fragmentation mentioned here reflects both the urgency and complexity of this transition, as companies across sectors scramble to future-proof their encryption against the looming threat of cryptographically relevant quantum computers. This ecosystem expansion signals that quantum-safe migration has moved from experimental phase to business-critical deployment, making this a crucial reference for understanding who's driving the post-quantum cryptography revolution.
