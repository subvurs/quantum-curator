SEALSQ Advances Satellite-Based Post-Quantum Security Platform

Source: The Quantum Insider June 12, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

SEALSQ's Quantum Spatial Orbital Cloud represents a fascinating convergence of post-quantum cryptography and space-based infrastructure, addressing the urgent need for quantum-resistant security systems before large-scale quantum computers threaten current encryption standards. This satellite-based approach is particularly intriguing because it could provide quantum-secure communications to regions with limited terrestrial infrastructure while potentially offering enhanced security through the physical isolation of space-based systems. The timing is strategic, as organizations worldwide are racing to implement post-quantum cryptographic solutions ahead of the anticipated "Y2Q" moment when quantum computers will break today's encryption methods. While still in development, this orbital security platform illustrates how the post-quantum transition is driving innovation across multiple technology domains beyond just algorithm development.

— Mark Eatherly
