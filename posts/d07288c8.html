Source: The Quantum Insider March 24, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article highlights how quantum computing is increasingly viewed through the lens of national security and international competition, with policymakers recognizing that quantum supremacy could reshape global power dynamics. Tugendhat's proposal for deeper U.K.-U.S. quantum collaboration reflects growing concerns about China's substantial investments in quantum research and the strategic importance of maintaining Western leadership in this critical technology. The mention of Quantinuum as a model partnership underscores how the quantum industry is already operating across borders, suggesting that formal government partnerships could amplify existing commercial and research ties. This signals a shift from viewing quantum computing as purely a scientific endeavor to recognizing it as a cornerstone of future economic and military competitiveness.
