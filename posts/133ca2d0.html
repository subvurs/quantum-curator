Rigetti Simulates Plasma Wave Dispersion on Superconducting Ankaa-3 Processor Using Specialized Error Mitigation

Source: Quantum Computing Report May 30, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This collaboration between Rigetti, Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, and the University of Colorado represents a significant step toward practical quantum simulations of complex physical phenomena, specifically demonstrating how quantum computers can model electromagnetic wave behavior in plasma environments. The use of specialized error mitigation techniques on Rigetti's Ankaa-3 superconducting processor is particularly noteworthy, as plasma physics simulations are notoriously computationally intensive and require high fidelity to produce meaningful results. This work could have important implications for fusion energy research and space physics applications, where understanding plasma wave dispersion is crucial for reactor design and satellite communications. The successful demonstration also validates the potential of near-term quantum devices to tackle real-world scientific computing problems when paired with sophisticated error correction methods.

— Mark Eatherly
