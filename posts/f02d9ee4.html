Quantinuum to Debut on Nasdaq After Raising $1.68 Billion in IPO

Source: The Quantum Insider June 04, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This IPO represents a watershed moment for the quantum computing industry, as Quantinuum becomes one of the first major pure-play quantum companies to go public with such substantial investor backing. The $1.68 billion raise at $60 per share signals growing institutional confidence in quantum computing's commercial viability, particularly given Quantinuum's unique position as the merger of Honeywell Quantum Solutions and Cambridge Quantum Computing, combining hardware and software expertise. This public debut will likely serve as a benchmark for how investors value quantum companies and could pave the way for other quantum startups to access public markets. The success or struggles of Quantinuum's public performance will be closely watched as a barometer for the entire quantum computing sector's maturation from research curiosity to investable technology.

— Mark Eatherly
