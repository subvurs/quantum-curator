Infleqtion Awarded $2 Million DARPA Contract for Heterogeneous Quantum Software

Source: Quantum Computing Report April 21, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This DARPA contract highlights a critical but often overlooked challenge in quantum computing: creating software that can seamlessly orchestrate different types of quantum hardware working together. Infleqtion's Multistaq platform represents a significant step toward heterogeneous quantum systems, where neutral atoms might handle certain computational tasks while superconducting qubits tackle others, all within a single workflow. This approach could unlock powerful hybrid architectures that leverage the unique strengths of each qubit technology, potentially accelerating practical quantum applications in defense and beyond. The military's investment signals growing recognition that quantum advantage may emerge not from any single hardware platform, but from intelligently combining multiple quantum technologies.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Infleqtion has secured a $2 million contract from the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) to support the Heterogeneous Architectures for Quantum (HARQ) program. The 24-month project focuses on the development of Multistaq, a software platform designed to manage quantum systems that integrate multiple qubit modalities, such as neutral atoms, trapped ions, and superconducting circuits.
