William Phillips: Nobel laureate talks about his passion for quantum physics

Source: Physics World - Quantum April 04, 2025 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This interview with Nobel Prize winner William Phillips offers valuable perspective from one of the foundational figures in quantum technology, whose pioneering work in laser cooling of atoms laid crucial groundwork for today's quantum computing and sensing applications. Phillips' insights are particularly relevant as the field grapples with scaling challenges, since his techniques for precisely controlling atomic motion remain central to trapped-ion quantum computers and atomic clocks that serve as quantum sensors. The article provides readers with a rare glimpse into how a quantum physics pioneer views the current state and future potential of the technologies his research helped make possible. Given Phillips' decades of experience bridging fundamental physics and practical applications, his thoughts on where quantum technologies are headed carry significant weight in understanding the field's trajectory.

— Mark Eatherly
