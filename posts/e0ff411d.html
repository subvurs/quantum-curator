Phasecraft Appointed by ARPA-E to Advance Catalyst Simulation Frameworks for Hydrogen Electrolysis

Source: Quantum Computing Report June 16, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This ARPA-E award represents a significant validation of quantum computing's potential to tackle real-world energy challenges, specifically optimizing catalysts for green hydrogen production, which is crucial for decarbonization efforts. Phasecraft's selection under the competitive QC3 program underscores a strategic shift in government funding toward quantum applications with near-term industrial impact rather than purely theoretical research. The substantial $4.5 million investment signals growing confidence that quantum algorithms are mature enough to address the complex chemistry simulations needed for catalyst design, which remains computationally intractable for classical systems. This project could serve as an important test case for whether current and near-term quantum hardware can deliver practical advantages in materials discovery for clean energy technologies.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Quantum software company Phasecraft Inc. has secured a $4,519,658 contract from the U.S. Department of Energy’s Advanced Research Projects Agency-Energy (ARPA-E). Awarded under the selective Quantum Computing for Computational Chemistry (QC3) program, the initiative is structured to develop, translate, and run highly optimized quantum simulation algorithms on emerging hardware.
