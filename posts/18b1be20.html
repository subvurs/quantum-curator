QPerfect Partners with University of Strasbourg on Neutral-Atom Quantum Computing Platform

Source: The Quantum Insider July 22, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

AI Commentary

This article marks a notable step toward democratizing neutral‑atom quantum hardware by linking BTQ’s QPerfect unit with the University of Strasbourg to develop France’s first publicly accessible aQCess platform. By combining a commercial security‑focused firm with academic expertise, the collaboration could accelerate the deployment of scalable, room‑temperature atom arrays that are well suited for cloud‑based quantum services and error‑mitigation research. If successful, it would complement recent advances from companies like Pasqal and QuEra, helping to broaden Europe’s neutral‑atom ecosystem while still leaving open questions about performance benchmarks and integration with existing software stacks.

— Mark Eatherly
