International scientists head into the fast-lane of Denmark’s burgeoning quantum ecosystem

Source: Physics World - Quantum March 17, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

Denmark is emerging as a significant player in the global quantum talent race, strategically positioning itself to compete with quantum powerhouses like the US, China, and other European nations. This influx of international scientists signals that smaller countries can build competitive quantum ecosystems through focused investment and smart policy frameworks, rather than relying solely on massive funding scales. The trend reflects a broader shift where quantum expertise is becoming increasingly distributed globally, creating new opportunities for international collaboration and potentially accelerating overall progress in the field. Denmark's success in attracting top talent could serve as a blueprint for other nations looking to establish their own quantum research hubs and avoid being left behind in this critical technology race.

— Mark Eatherly
