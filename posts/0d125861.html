Washington Begins to Treat Quantum Technology as a Strategic Priority

Source: The Quantum Insider March 25, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article signals a critical inflection point where quantum computing is transitioning from academic curiosity to national security imperative in Washington policy circles. The shift toward treating quantum as a strategic priority reflects growing recognition that the technology's potential advantages in cryptography, optimization, and simulation could reshape economic and military balance of power globally. The mentioned "quiet frustration" likely stems from the gap between quantum's theoretical promise and current hardware limitations, as policymakers grapple with funding decisions for a technology still years away from broad practical deployment. This policy momentum could accelerate both public investment and regulatory frameworks that will ultimately determine whether the U.S. maintains its competitive edge in the emerging quantum landscape.

— Mark Eatherly
