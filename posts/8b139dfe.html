Pasqal and True Nexus Collaborate on Quantum Modeling for Food Proteins

Source: The Quantum Insider April 11, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This collaboration between Pasqal and True Nexus represents an intriguing application of neutral-atom quantum computing to one of biology's most complex challenges: understanding protein behavior in food systems. While quantum advantage for protein modeling remains largely theoretical, this partnership signals growing industry confidence that near-term quantum computers could tackle specific aspects of protein folding and functionality that are computationally intensive for classical systems. The timing is notable as Pasqal prepares for its public debut, suggesting the company is positioning quantum computing as a practical tool for biotechnology applications rather than just a research curiosity. Food protein optimization could become one of the earlier commercial use cases for quantum computing, potentially impacting everything from plant-based meat alternatives to food preservation technologies.

— Mark Eatherly
