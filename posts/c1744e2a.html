Toshiba and LQUOM Collaborate on Long-Distance Quantum Repeater Research

Source: Quantum Computing Report March 28, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This collaboration between Toshiba and LQUOM tackles one of quantum cryptography's most pressing challenges: extending the reach of quantum key distribution beyond the current limitations of fiber optic transmission, which typically degrades quantum signals after a few hundred kilometers. Quantum repeaters represent a crucial stepping stone toward building a global quantum internet, as they can amplify and relay quantum information without destroying its delicate quantum properties. The partnership combines Toshiba's proven QKD expertise with LQUOM's specialized quantum memory and repeater technology, potentially bringing us closer to secure quantum communication networks that could span continents. While the research timeline extends to 2027, success could dramatically accelerate the deployment of quantum-secured communications for critical infrastructure and international data transmission.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Toshiba Corporation and LQUOM Inc. have entered into a joint research agreement to study the extension of Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) ranges using quantum repeater technology. The project, scheduled to run from March 2026 to March 2027, focuses on evaluating the technical feasibility of integrating Toshiba’s QKD systems with LQUOM’s quantum repeater architectures.
