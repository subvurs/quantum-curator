Silicon Quantum Computing Secures Additional AUD$40 Million From Australia’s NRFC

Source: The Quantum Insider June 11, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This substantial funding boost for Silicon Quantum Computing represents Australia's strategic commitment to maintaining a competitive edge in the global quantum race, particularly in silicon-based quantum processors that could leverage existing semiconductor infrastructure. The AUD$60 million total investment from Australia's National Reconstruction Fund Corporation signals government recognition that quantum computing is not just a research curiosity but a critical technology for national competitiveness and security. SQC's silicon spin qubit approach offers potential advantages in manufacturability and scalability compared to other qubit technologies, making this investment particularly noteworthy as the field moves toward practical quantum systems. This funding pattern reflects a broader trend of nations making significant sovereign investments in quantum technology to avoid dependence on foreign quantum capabilities.

— Mark Eatherly
