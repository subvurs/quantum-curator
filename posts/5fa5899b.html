Quantum Elements, Rigetti Computing Exploring New Digital Twin Simulation Approach

Source: The Quantum Insider April 21, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This collaboration between Quantum Elements and Rigetti represents an intriguing convergence of AI-powered digital twins with quantum computing hardware, potentially offering a new pathway to extract practical value from today's noisy intermediate-scale quantum devices. Digital twin technology could help bridge the gap between theoretical quantum algorithms and real-world applications by providing high-fidelity simulations that account for hardware limitations and environmental noise. The partnership highlights how quantum software companies are increasingly focusing on hybrid classical-quantum approaches that leverage AI to optimize quantum circuit performance and identify use cases where current quantum hardware can deliver genuine advantages. If successful, this approach could accelerate the timeline for achieving quantum utility in specific application domains before full fault-tolerant quantum computers become available.

— Mark Eatherly
