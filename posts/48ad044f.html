How to Benchmark Logical Qubit Claims

Source: The Quantum Insider June 08, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article tackles a crucial shift happening in quantum computing evaluation as the field matures beyond simple qubit counting. As companies race to demonstrate fault-tolerant quantum computers, the focus is rightfully moving from raw physical qubits to logical qubits that can actually perform error-corrected computation reliably. The challenge lies in establishing standardized benchmarks for logical qubit performance, since different approaches to quantum error correction make direct comparisons between systems increasingly complex. This shift in measurement philosophy reflects the quantum industry's transition from proof-of-concept demonstrations to building machines that can solve real-world problems.

— Mark Eatherly
