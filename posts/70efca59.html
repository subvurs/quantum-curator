QSE Launches Enterprise Post-Quantum Migration Platform with QPA v2 Release

Source: Quantum Computing Report April 07, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article signals a crucial shift from talking about post-quantum cryptography to actually deploying it at enterprise scale. While much of the quantum security discussion has focused on when quantum computers will break current encryption, QSE's automated migration platform addresses the more immediate challenge of how organizations will practically transition their systems before that threat materializes. The move from manual, piecemeal approaches to comprehensive automation platforms suggests the post-quantum cryptography market is maturing rapidly, driven by increasing awareness that migration timelines may be longer and more complex than initially anticipated. This type of enterprise tooling could prove essential as organizations face the daunting task of inventorying and upgrading cryptographic infrastructure across potentially thousands of systems and applications.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Quantum Secure Encryption Corp. (QSE) has announced the official launch of QPA v2, an enterprise-grade platform designed to automate and manage the migration to post-quantum cryptography (PQC). The release signifies a transition within the cybersecurity market from theoretical awareness of quantum risks toward the structured execution of cryptographic modernization.
