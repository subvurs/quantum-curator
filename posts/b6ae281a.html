Pasqal Launches Italy’s First Neutral-Atom Quantum Computer at CINECA

Source: The Quantum Insider June 11, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This marks a significant milestone for European quantum infrastructure as Italy joins the growing network of nations deploying neutral-atom quantum computers for research and development. Pasqal's expansion into Italy through CINECA represents more than just geographic diversification - it demonstrates how quantum computing is becoming integrated into existing high-performance computing ecosystems, where quantum systems can eventually work alongside classical supercomputers. The partnership with Italy's largest public supercomputing operator signals that European research institutions are moving beyond experimental quantum projects toward building practical quantum-classical hybrid computing capabilities. This deployment also strengthens Europe's position in the global quantum race, particularly in neutral-atom technology where Pasqal competes directly with American companies like QuEra and Atom Computing.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Insider Brief PRESS RELEASE — Pasqal, one of the global leaders in neutral-atom quantum computing, today announced the inauguration of Europe’s third Pasqal quantum computer hosted at CINECA, Italy’s largest public supercomputing operator and a member of the Italian Research Center on High Performance Computing (ICSC), Big Data, and Quantum Computing, in Bologna, Italy.
