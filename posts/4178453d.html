Q-Factor Emerges from Stealth with $24M Seed Round to Scale Neutral Atom Systems

Source: Quantum Computing Report April 06, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article highlights the growing momentum behind neutral atom quantum computing architectures, with Q-Factor's ambitious goal of scaling to a million qubits representing one of the most aggressive roadmaps in the field. The $24M seed round signals strong investor confidence in neutral atoms as a viable path to large-scale quantum systems, particularly noteworthy given Intel Capital's participation, which suggests established tech giants are hedging their bets beyond traditional superconducting and trapped ion approaches. While a million-qubit system remains a distant goal fraught with technical challenges around error rates and coherence, Q-Factor's entry adds valuable competition to a space currently dominated by companies like QuEra and Pasqal, potentially accelerating progress in this promising quantum computing modality.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Q-Factor, a Tel Aviv-based quantum hardware startup, has exited stealth with $24 million (USD) in seed funding to develop a million-qubit neutral atom quantum computer.
