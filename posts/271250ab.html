Guest Post: Why Europe’s Quantum Future Depends on Talent Hubs, Not Just Tech Milestones

Source: The Quantum Insider May 16, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This guest post from Quantum Machines CEO Itamar Sivan shifts focus from the typical hardware metrics to a crucial but often overlooked challenge facing Europe's quantum ambitions: building robust talent ecosystems. While much attention centers on qubit counts and error rates, Sivan argues that Europe's quantum leadership will ultimately depend on creating thriving hubs where top researchers, engineers, and entrepreneurs can collaborate and innovate together. This perspective is particularly timely as European nations pour billions into quantum initiatives through programs like Quantum Flagship, yet still struggle to retain talent against competition from Silicon Valley and other global tech centers. The article highlights a strategic blind spot that could determine whether Europe's substantial quantum investments translate into actual technological and commercial leadership.

— Mark Eatherly
