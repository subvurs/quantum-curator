Pasqal Selected as Finalist in XPRIZE Quantum Applications Competition

Source: The Quantum Insider May 20, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

Pasqal's selection as an XPRIZE Quantum Applications finalist highlights the growing recognition of neutral-atom quantum computing as a viable path to quantum advantage, validating their unique approach alongside more established qubit technologies. The timing is particularly notable as Pasqal prepares to go public, positioning the French startup to compete directly with major players like IBM and Google in the race to demonstrate practical quantum applications. This recognition underscores how the quantum computing landscape is diversifying beyond superconducting and trapped-ion systems, with neutral atoms offering distinct advantages in scalability and connectivity that could prove crucial for certain quantum algorithms. The XPRIZE competition's focus on real-world applications rather than abstract benchmarks also signals the field's maturation toward solving actual problems rather than just achieving technical milestones.

— Mark Eatherly
