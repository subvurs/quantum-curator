Dynamics of entanglement entropy for a locally monitored lattice gauge theory

Source: arXiv Quantum Physics March 31, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This research tackles a fundamental challenge in quantum simulation of gauge theories - understanding how continuous measurements affect quantum entanglement in these complex systems. The finding that local monitoring doesn't trigger measurement-induced phase transitions is particularly significant because it suggests that certain quantum simulations of particle physics models can maintain their coherent properties even under realistic experimental conditions where measurements are unavoidable. This work provides crucial benchmarking insights for quantum computers attempting to simulate lattice gauge theories, which represent some of the most promising applications for demonstrating quantum advantage in fundamental physics research. The results help establish the robustness of these quantum simulations against measurement-induced decoherence, bringing us closer to reliable quantum exploration of high-energy physics phenomena.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

The $1+1$ dimensional $Z_2$ gauge theory is the simplest model that allows for quantum computation or quantum simulation to probe the fundamental aspects of a gauge theory coupled with dynamical fermions. To reliably benchmark such a system, it is crucial to understand the non-unitary quantum dynamics arising from the underlying non-Hermitian evolution and to model the effects of quantum measurements. This work focuses on monitoring ultra-local physical observables for a $\mathbb Z_2$ gauge theory. Tensor network calculations are performed to dynamically probe entanglement entropy at larger lattice sizes. In this work, we report that continuously monitoring local and diagonal observables (electric and mass energy densities) in the computational basis demonstrates the absence of any measurement-induced phase transition, as indicated by the system-size independence of the late-time saturation value of the bipartite entanglement entropy.
