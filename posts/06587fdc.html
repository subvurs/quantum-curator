Open Quantum Design, Western Digital, and QuScript Form Open-Source Error Correction Working Group

Source: Quantum Computing Report March 26, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This collaboration represents a significant shift toward open-source development in quantum error correction, an area traditionally dominated by proprietary research at major tech companies. By combining Open Quantum Design's hardware expertise, Western Digital's storage and memory technologies, and QuScript's software capabilities, this working group could accelerate progress in quantum error correction by making both the hardware designs and software stack publicly available. The open-source approach is particularly valuable for QEC development since it allows the broader research community to experiment with different error correction codes and implementations without building everything from scratch. This initiative could democratize access to fault-tolerant quantum computing research and potentially speed up the timeline for achieving practical quantum error correction systems.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Open Quantum Design (OQD), Western Digital (WD), and QuScript have established a collaborative "Error Correction working group" to develop a full-stack, open-source quantum computer prototype. The initiative aims to provide an end-to-end platform for demonstrating quantum error correction (QEC), a critical requirement for achieving fault-tolerant quantum computing.
