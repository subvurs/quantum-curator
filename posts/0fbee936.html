A Class of Multipartite Entangled States Based on State Transitions

Source: arXiv Quantum Physics June 04, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This research introduces a fresh perspective on multipartite entanglement by defining quantum states based on "transitions" between adjacent qubits rather than the traditional approaches of global correlations or excitation counts. The key insight that these Transition states can be converted to well-studied Dicke states using standard controlled-X gates creates an intriguing bridge between different mathematical frameworks for understanding many-body entanglement. This work could prove valuable for quantum algorithm designers and experimentalists, as it provides a new toolkit for generating and manipulating complex entangled states that might be more naturally suited to certain quantum computing architectures or applications. While the immediate practical impact remains to be seen, establishing these fundamental connections between different classes of multipartite entangled states strengthens our theoretical foundation for quantum information processing.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

We introduce Transition states (T states), denoted by $\ket{T_k^n}$, as a class of multipartite entangled states characterized by a fixed number of state transitions between adjacent qubits. These states form equal-amplitude superpositions over all states with a specified transition count. Unlike Bell states based on two-qubit correlations, GHZ states characterized by global correlations among all qubits, and W and Dicke states based on fixed numbers of qubit excitations, T states are defined by transition counts along an ordered sequence of qubits. We prove that T states are unitarily equivalent to Dicke states through a chain of CX (controlled-X) operations, thereby establishing a direct correspondence between transition-based and excitation-based representations of multipartite entanglement.
