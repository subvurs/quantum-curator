Quandela and Safran Partner on Quantum Fluid Simulation Research

Source: The Quantum Insider May 07, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This collaboration between Quandela and aerospace giant Safran represents a significant step toward practical quantum applications in computational fluid dynamics, one of the most computationally demanding problems in engineering. Fluid flow simulation around aircraft is notoriously complex, requiring massive computational resources that current classical computers struggle with, making it an ideal target for quantum advantage. The partnership is particularly noteworthy because it brings together Quandela's photonic quantum computing expertise with Safran's deep aerospace engineering knowledge, suggesting we're moving beyond theoretical quantum algorithms toward real-world industrial applications. If successful, this could accelerate aircraft design optimization and fuel efficiency improvements while demonstrating quantum computing's value in solving practical engineering challenges.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Insider Brief PRESS RELEASE — Quandela and Safran Tech, Safran’s research and technology centre, have announced the launch of the AQeFLU research project. This partnership aims to develop new quantum algorithms dedicated to the complex modelling of fluid flows.
