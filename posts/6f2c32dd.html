Western Digital Adds Post-Quantum Cryptography to Hard Drives

Source: The Quantum Insider May 19, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This announcement represents a crucial shift from quantum cryptography being a distant concern to an immediate infrastructure reality. Western Digital's integration of post-quantum cryptography into their high-capacity drives signals that major storage manufacturers are now actively preparing for the eventual arrival of cryptographically relevant quantum computers, which could render current encryption methods vulnerable. The move is particularly strategic given that data stored today on these drives may need to remain secure for decades, potentially outlasting the timeline for quantum computers capable of breaking current encryption standards. This proactive approach by a major hardware vendor demonstrates how the quantum threat is driving real-world cybersecurity adaptations across the technology stack, not just in theoretical research labs.

— Mark Eatherly
