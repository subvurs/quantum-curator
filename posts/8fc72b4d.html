Method for measuring energy amounts less than a trillionth of a billionth of a joule could boost quantum computing

Source: Phys.org Quantum May 12, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This breakthrough in ultra-precise energy measurement represents a significant leap forward for quantum sensing capabilities, potentially enabling detection of energy levels as small as 10^-21 joules—a sensitivity that could revolutionize both quantum computing and fundamental physics research. The ability to measure such minuscule energy amounts could dramatically improve the precision of quantum state control and readout, addressing one of the key challenges in building reliable quantum computers where even tiny energy fluctuations can destroy delicate quantum information. Beyond quantum computing applications, this level of sensitivity opens exciting possibilities for detecting hypothetical dark matter particles like axions, which would only interact with ordinary matter through extremely weak energy exchanges. The advancement showcases how improvements in quantum measurement technology often have dual benefits, enhancing both our technological capabilities and our ability to probe the deepest mysteries of the universe.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

The fundamentals of quantum mechanics are minuscule. Scientists constantly home in on finer resolutions to measure, quantify, and control these fundamentals, like photons that carry light and have no mass unless they are moving. The more precise the measurement, the more possibilities for better quantum technology or the ability to detect elusive dark-matter axions in deep space.
