Safe But Slow? Quantum-Safe Security Could Slow High-Speed Blockchains

Source: The Quantum Insider April 07, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article highlights a critical tension emerging across the cryptocurrency ecosystem as quantum computers inch closer to threatening current encryption methods. Solana's dilemma perfectly illustrates the broader challenge facing high-performance blockchain networks: the quantum-resistant cryptographic algorithms needed to protect against future quantum attacks are computationally heavier than today's vulnerable but lightweight encryption schemes. This trade-off between security and speed isn't unique to Solana and will likely force the entire crypto industry to carefully balance performance with quantum resilience in the coming years. The timing is particularly significant as NIST recently standardized post-quantum cryptographic algorithms, making these implementation decisions increasingly urgent for blockchain developers.

— Mark Eatherly
