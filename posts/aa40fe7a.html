Arkeon Lands Seed Investment to Boost Quantum Chip Production Yields

Source: The Quantum Insider June 12, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This seed funding for Arkeon highlights a critical but often overlooked challenge in quantum computing: manufacturing consistency. While much attention focuses on qubit counts and error rates, the reality is that superconducting quantum chips suffer from significant fabrication variations that can make identical designs perform vastly differently. Arkeon's approach to improving production yields addresses a fundamental bottleneck that could accelerate the path to commercially viable quantum computers by making high-quality chips more reliably manufacturable. The backing from Chalmers Ventures, connected to one of Europe's leading quantum research institutions, suggests this Swedish startup may have identified genuine solutions to problems that have plagued the industry since its early days.

— Mark Eatherly

Insider Brief Swedish startup Arkeon has raised €594,200 (SEK 6.5 million) in seed funding to advance a manufacturing technology designed to improve the consistency and performance of superconducting quantum chips, according to information reported by EU Startups.
