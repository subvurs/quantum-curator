SES and Airbus to Build Optical Ground Station for EAGLE-1 Quantum Satellite

Source: The Quantum Insider July 15, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

AI Commentary

This article matters because it marks the first dedicated optical ground station in Europe built specifically to receive photons from a quantum communications satellite, paving the way for large‑scale quantum key distribution across the continent. By locating the OGS at the NL Space Campus next to ESA’s ESTeC facility, SES and Airbus are leveraging existing space‑technology expertise to accelerate the transition from experimental demos like China’s Micius to operational, commercial quantum networks. The partnership signals growing industry confidence in satellite‑based quantum links, though practical rollout will still need to overcome atmospheric turbulence and integrate with terrestrial fiber backbones.

— Mark Eatherly
