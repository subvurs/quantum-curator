Prototype sets record for optical quantum information technology

Source: Phys.org Quantum May 18, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This latest iteration of China's Jiuzhang photonic quantum computer represents a significant milestone in the global race for quantum supremacy, building on the groundbreaking achievements of earlier versions that first demonstrated quantum advantage in 2020. The Jiuzhang series has consistently pushed the boundaries of what's possible with optical quantum computing, offering a compelling alternative to the superconducting and trapped-ion approaches favored by other quantum computing leaders. What makes this particularly noteworthy is the programmable aspect of Jiuzhang 4.0, which moves beyond specialized quantum sampling tasks toward more general-purpose quantum computation using photons. This achievement reinforces China's position as a major player in quantum technologies while advancing the field's understanding of how different physical platforms can be leveraged for quantum advantage.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Chinese scientists have developed a programmable quantum computing prototype called Jiuzhang 4.0 that has set a new world record for optical quantum information technology, according to a study published May 13 in the journal Nature.
