Alice & Bob Launches On-Premise “Helium Quantum System” for Cat-Qubit Research

Source: Quantum Computing Report June 11, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article marks a significant milestone for Alice & Bob as they evolve from a quantum chip manufacturer into a full-stack quantum computing company, joining the ranks of players like IBM and Rigetti who offer complete quantum systems. The Helium system's focus on cat-qubits represents a fascinating alternative approach to quantum error correction, as these "noise-biased" qubits are theoretically more resistant to certain types of errors that plague conventional superconducting qubits. By targeting their first logical qubit implementation with 18 physical cat-qubits, Alice & Bob is positioning itself at the forefront of the race toward practical fault-tolerant quantum computing, where logical qubits protected by quantum error correction will be essential for running useful algorithms. The move to on-premise systems also signals growing maturity in the quantum industry, as companies recognize that researchers need hands-on access to experiment with novel qubit architectures rather than relying solely on cloud access.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Fault-tolerant hardware developer Alice & Bob has unveiled the Helium Quantum System, transitioning the company from chip-level manufacturing into delivering full-stack, on-premise quantum computing platforms. Operating on a noise-biased cat-qubit architecture, the integrated machine is engineered to encode the company's first logical qubit using an array of 18 physical cat-qubits.
