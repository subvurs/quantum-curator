New entanglement approach could boost photonic quantum computing

Source: Physics World - Quantum April 09, 2025 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This theoretical breakthrough could address one of photonic quantum computing's biggest headaches: the probabilistic nature of photon entanglement that has plagued efforts to scale up light-based quantum systems. By leveraging non-Abelian holonomy - essentially exploiting how quantum states change when moved around closed paths in parameter space - researchers propose a way to create entangled photon pairs deterministically rather than relying on the hit-or-miss approaches that currently dominate the field. This matters enormously because deterministic entanglement could dramatically improve the success rates of photonic quantum gates, potentially making room-temperature photonic quantum computers more viable competitors to their superconducting cousins. While still theoretical, this approach represents the kind of mathematical elegance that often translates into practical quantum computing advantages down the road.

— Mark Eatherly
