SEEQC Secures Role in CHIPS Act-Funded NORDTECH Quantum Scaling Project

Source: Quantum Computing Report June 16, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

SEEQC's inclusion in the CHIPS Act-funded NORDTECH program represents a strategic convergence of quantum computing development with domestic semiconductor manufacturing priorities. This collaboration brings together academic heavyweights like Cornell and Princeton with commercial players including D-Wave, creating an ecosystem focused on the critical challenge of scaling quantum systems through advanced microelectronics integration. The involvement underscores how quantum computing is increasingly viewed as a national security priority requiring the same manufacturing infrastructure investments as classical computing. For SEEQC specifically, this validates their digital quantum computing approach that relies heavily on sophisticated cryogenic chip fabrication, positioning them alongside established quantum players in accessing federal research funding and fabrication resources.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Digital quantum hardware developer SEEQC, Inc. has announced its participation as a subcontractor in the multi-year Microelectronics Commons Northeast Regional Defense Technology Hub (NORDTECH) program.
