Can quantum computers now solve health care problems? We’ll soon find out.

Source: MIT Technology Review March 19, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article highlights a crucial transition point where quantum computing moves from theoretical promise to real-world testing in healthcare applications. The UK's National Quantum Computing Centre represents a significant investment in bridging the gap between laboratory demonstrations and practical problem-solving, particularly in areas like drug discovery and medical optimization where quantum advantages could genuinely transform outcomes. What makes this development particularly noteworthy is the focus on neutral atom quantum computers, which offer unique advantages for certain types of calculations compared to the more commonly discussed superconducting systems. The emphasis on healthcare applications is strategic, as this sector presents some of the most compelling near-term use cases where quantum computing's ability to handle complex molecular interactions could deliver measurable benefits well before fault-tolerant quantum computers become widely available.

— Mark Eatherly
