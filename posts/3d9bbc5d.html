Rigetti Sells Novera QPU to University of Saskatchewan for Canada’s First Open-Architecture System

Source: Quantum Computing Report April 04, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This sale represents a significant shift toward democratizing quantum computing access through on-premises university installations, marking Canada's entry into the growing trend of academic institutions owning their own quantum systems rather than relying solely on cloud access. The open-architecture design of Rigetti's Novera QPU is particularly noteworthy as it allows researchers to experiment with the full quantum stack, from hardware control to algorithm development, fostering deeper understanding and innovation that's often constrained in cloud-only environments. This milestone reflects the maturing quantum hardware market, where companies like Rigetti are finding sustainable business models by selling smaller, more accessible systems to educational institutions that can serve as training grounds for the next generation of quantum researchers and engineers.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Rigetti Computing (Nasdaq: RGTI) has officially expanded its on-premises footprint with the sale of a 9-qubit Novera™ QPU to the University of Saskatchewan (USask). This acquisition marks a historic milestone as USask becomes home to the first university-owned and operated, vendor-supported, full-stack, open-architecture quantum computer in Canada.
