Chinese Quantum Startup QBoson Raises $145 Million to Scale Chip Production

Source: The Quantum Insider April 06, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This $145 million funding round for QBoson represents a significant milestone in China's aggressive push to commercialize quantum computing, moving beyond purely academic research toward industrial-scale manufacturing. The substantial investment underscores Beijing's strategic commitment to quantum supremacy and suggests Chinese companies are preparing to compete directly with Western firms like IBM, Google, and IonQ in the global quantum market. QBoson's focus on scaling chip production is particularly noteworthy, as manufacturing quantum processors at volume remains one of the field's biggest bottlenecks - if they can crack this challenge, it could dramatically accelerate quantum adoption across industries. This funding also signals that private investors are increasingly confident in quantum computing's near-term commercial viability, despite the technology's current limitations.

— Mark Eatherly
