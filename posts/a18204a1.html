Classiq and UC Chile Launch Quantum Computing Research for Biomedical Imaging

Source: The Quantum Insider June 05, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This collaboration between Classiq and UC Chile represents a promising convergence of quantum computing with practical healthcare applications, specifically targeting the computationally intensive challenge of medical image analysis where quantum algorithms could potentially offer significant advantages. The project's hybrid approach, combining quantum algorithms with classical machine learning on NVIDIA's CUDA-Q platform, exemplifies the current industry trend toward near-term quantum-classical systems that can deliver practical benefits before fault-tolerant quantum computers arrive. Biomedical imaging is particularly well-suited for quantum enhancement given the complex pattern recognition and optimization problems involved in pathology analysis, making this a strategic research direction that could accelerate both quantum algorithm development and medical diagnostics capabilities.

— Mark Eatherly

Insider Brief PRESS RELEASE — Classiq and Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile (UC Chile) today announced a joint research project to develop hybrid quantum algorithms for biomedical image analysis, assisted by classical machine learning and the NVIDIA CUDA-Q platform for quantum-classical computing.
