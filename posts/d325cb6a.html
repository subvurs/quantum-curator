Silent Waves Introduces Integrated Cryogenic Amplifier Zephyr

Source: The Quantum Insider March 26, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This announcement represents a significant step toward more practical quantum computing systems, as cryogenic amplifiers like the Zephyr are essential for reading out quantum states while operating at the millikelvin temperatures required by superconducting qubits. Silent Waves' integration of the traveling-wave parametric amplifier and pump coupler into a single component addresses a key engineering challenge in quantum systems - reducing complexity and potential failure points in the delicate cryogenic environment. The compact design could help quantum computing companies streamline their hardware architectures and potentially improve system reliability, which is crucial as the field moves from laboratory demonstrations toward commercial quantum computers. While cryogenic amplifiers may seem like a niche component, they're actually critical infrastructure that enables the precise quantum state measurements that make quantum computing possible.

— Mark Eatherly
