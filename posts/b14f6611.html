Chinese Researchers Demonstrate Logical Operations on Silicon Quantum Processor

Source: The Quantum Insider March 28, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This demonstration represents a crucial milestone in making quantum computing practically viable by proving that error correction can work within silicon-based systems that leverage existing semiconductor manufacturing infrastructure. The breakthrough from Shenzhen International Quantum Academy is particularly significant because it bridges the gap between laboratory quantum experiments and scalable manufacturing, potentially accelerating the path to fault-tolerant quantum computers without requiring entirely new fabrication facilities. While many quantum computing approaches rely on exotic materials or extreme conditions, silicon-based systems offer the promise of tapping into decades of semiconductor industry expertise and established supply chains. This work suggests that the quantum computing revolution might unfold within familiar technological frameworks rather than requiring a complete reinvention of how we build processors.

— Mark Eatherly
