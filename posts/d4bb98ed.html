Modelling wavefunction collapse as a continuous flow yields insights on the nature of measurement

Source: Physics World - Quantum January 22, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article explores a fascinating new approach to understanding one of quantum mechanics' most puzzling phenomena - how quantum states "collapse" during measurement. By treating wavefunction collapse as a continuous flow process rather than an instantaneous event, researchers are applying quantum state diffusion theory to characterize measurements through entropy production, potentially offering fresh insights into the fundamental mechanism that bridges quantum superposition and classical reality. This work could have significant implications for quantum sensing applications, where understanding the precise dynamics of measurement processes is crucial for optimizing detector sensitivity and minimizing decoherence. The research represents an important step toward resolving long-standing questions about the measurement problem in quantum mechanics while potentially enabling more sophisticated quantum sensing technologies.

— Mark Eatherly
