An integrated ultrahigh vacuum cluster tool for diamond surface science and single nitrogen-vacancy center measurements

Source: arXiv Quantum Physics June 11, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article presents a significant advance in nitrogen-vacancy (NV) center research by creating an integrated system that can modify diamond surfaces and immediately measure how those changes affect individual NV centers without exposing the sample to air contamination. The ability to directly correlate surface chemistry with NV quantum properties addresses one of the biggest challenges in quantum sensing - understanding and controlling how surface conditions affect the performance of these atomic-scale quantum sensors. This integrated platform could accelerate the development of more reliable quantum sensors by enabling researchers to systematically engineer diamond surfaces that protect NV centers from decoherence while maintaining their sensitivity. The work represents an important step toward making NV-based quantum sensors more practical for real-world applications where surface stability is crucial.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

We present a custom-designed ultrahigh vacuum (UHV) cluster tool developed for studying shallow nitrogen-vacancy (NV) centers in diamond, enabling in situ diamond surface preparation, characterization, and single NV center dynamics measurements within a single connected platform. The system combines a surface science chamber for controlled surface modification and analysis with a cryogenic confocal microscope chamber dedicated to NV spin and optical measurements. This integrated approach enables a direct correlation between diamond surface chemistry and the resulting NV spin and charge properties. The instrument provides a versatile platform for systematic studies of surface-induced decoherence mechanisms and charge dynamics for shallow NV centers, and establishes a pathway toward reproducible surface engineering for quantum sensing applications.
