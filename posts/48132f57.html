Rigetti Announces General Availability of 108-Qubit System

Source: The Quantum Insider April 08, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This announcement marks a significant milestone for Rigetti as they join the exclusive club of companies offering quantum processors with over 100 qubits, putting them in direct competition with IBM's larger systems while maintaining their distinctive superconducting architecture. The dual availability through both Rigetti's own QCS platform and Amazon Braket democratizes access to this higher-qubit-count system, potentially accelerating research and development across academia and industry. While raw qubit count alone doesn't determine quantum advantage, having 108 qubits available commercially gives researchers more room to explore error correction schemes and larger-scale quantum algorithms that simply weren't feasible on smaller systems. The move signals Rigetti's commitment to scaling their technology and staying competitive in the rapidly evolving quantum hardware landscape.

— Mark Eatherly
