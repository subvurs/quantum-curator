EPB Joins Southeastern Quantum Collaborative to Support Regional Infrastructure Integration

Source: Quantum Computing Report April 01, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This partnership between EPB and the Southeastern Quantum Collaborative represents an important step toward building the telecommunications infrastructure that quantum networks will eventually require. EPB, known for its advanced fiber optic network in Chattanooga, brings critical connectivity expertise to a collaborative that already includes major quantum companies like IBM, D-Wave, and IonQ. The move signals growing recognition that quantum computing's future depends not just on advancing the technology itself, but on developing the regional partnerships and infrastructure needed to support quantum applications at scale. As quantum technologies mature, collaboratives like SQC could become essential hubs for integrating quantum capabilities into existing business and research ecosystems across different regions.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

EPB has joined the Southeastern Quantum Collaborative (SQC) as an inaugural member. Led by the University of Alabama in Huntsville (UAH), the SQC is a regional association of academic, industrial, and government organizations—including IBM, D-Wave, and IonQ—tasked with coordinating quantum information science research and workforce development across the Southeast.
