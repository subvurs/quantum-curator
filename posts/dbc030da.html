The Companies Building Quantum Computing Chips in 2026

Source: The Quantum Insider June 05, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article captures a fascinating moment in quantum computing where the field has matured beyond academic research labs but hasn't yet converged on a single dominant technology platform, much like the early days of personal computing when dozens of incompatible architectures competed for market share. The diversity of approaches being pursued commercially in 2026 reflects both the enormous technical challenges of building fault-tolerant quantum computers and the genuine uncertainty about which physical implementation will ultimately prove most practical for real-world applications. Each modality brings unique advantages and trade-offs, from the fast gate operations of superconducting qubits to the long coherence times of trapped ions, creating a rich competitive landscape that should accelerate innovation across all platforms. This technological pluralism is actually healthy for the field, as it ensures multiple pathways toward the holy grail of fault-tolerant quantum computing rather than betting everything on a single approach.

— Mark Eatherly
