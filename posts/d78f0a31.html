Xanadu Launches Public Cloud Access for Borealis

Source: The Quantum Insider June 02, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article marks a significant milestone as Xanadu becomes the first company to offer public cloud access to a quantum computer that has demonstrated quantum computational advantage, breaking the traditional barrier between research achievements and practical accessibility. The Borealis system's photonic architecture represents a compelling alternative to the superconducting and trapped-ion approaches that dominate current quantum cloud offerings, potentially offering advantages in operating temperature and network connectivity for future quantum internet applications. What makes this particularly noteworthy is the full programmability of all gates, giving researchers unprecedented flexibility to explore quantum algorithms on a platform that has already proven its computational superiority over classical systems. This democratization of quantum advantage hardware could accelerate algorithm development and bring us closer to discovering the first commercially viable quantum applications.

— Mark Eatherly
