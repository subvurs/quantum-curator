Thermalization of SU(2) Lattice Gauge Fields on Quantum Computers

Source: arXiv Quantum Physics March 25, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This research marks a significant milestone in quantum simulation by successfully demonstrating thermalization dynamics of SU(2) gauge theory on real quantum hardware with up to 151 plaquettes, showing that current noisy quantum computers can meaningfully study complex many-body physics beyond what was previously thought feasible. The achievement is particularly noteworthy because gauge theories underpin our understanding of fundamental forces in physics, yet their quantum simulation has been extremely challenging due to their mathematical complexity and hardware requirements. By successfully matching quantum hardware results with classical simulations after error mitigation, the team has validated a pathway for using near-term quantum devices to explore chaotic quantum systems that could eventually become intractable for classical computers. This work bridges theoretical quantum field theory with practical quantum computing, opening doors for future studies of thermalization in more complex gauge theories relevant to particle physics and condensed matter systems.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

We simulate the thermalization dynamics for minimally truncated SU(2) pure gauge theory on linear plaquette chains with up to 151 plaquettes using IBM quantum computers. We study the time dependence of the entanglement spectrum, Rényi-2 entropy and anti-flatness on small subsystems. The quantum hardware results obtained after error mitigation agree with extrapolated classical simulator results for chains consisting of up to 101 plaquettes. Our results demonstrate the feasibility of local thermalization studies for chaotic quantum systems, such as nonabelian lattice gauge theories, on current noisy quantum computing platforms.
