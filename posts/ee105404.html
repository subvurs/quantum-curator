Quantum computers extend lead over classical machines in random circuit sampling

Source: Physics World - Quantum March 25, 2025 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This latest achievement with the Zuchongzhi 3.0 processor represents another significant milestone in demonstrating quantum supremacy, showing that quantum computers continue to widen their advantage over classical systems in specific computational tasks. Random circuit sampling, while not immediately practical, serves as a crucial benchmark for proving quantum computers can outperform even the world's most powerful supercomputers by astronomical margins - in this case, reducing billions of years of classical computation to mere minutes. The result reinforces that we're witnessing genuine quantum advantage in action and validates the rapid progress being made in quantum hardware development, particularly in photonic and superconducting quantum systems. While these demonstrations don't yet translate to solving real-world problems faster than classical computers, they provide essential proof that quantum computing is moving from theoretical possibility to computational reality.

— Mark Eatherly
