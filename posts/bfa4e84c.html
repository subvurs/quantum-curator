Microsoft’s Chetan Nayak on topological qubits, the physics of bigger splashes

Source: Physics World - Quantum March 27, 2025 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This Physics World podcast with Microsoft's Chetan Nayak offers valuable insights into one of quantum computing's most ambitious hardware approaches - topological qubits based on Majorana zero modes. Microsoft has been pursuing this challenging but potentially revolutionary path for over a decade, betting that topological protection could provide inherently error-resistant qubits that would dramatically reduce the overhead needed for quantum error correction. While Microsoft's topological quantum computing program has faced significant technical hurdles and timeline delays, recent progress in materials science and fabrication techniques has renewed optimism about this approach. The discussion likely provides important updates on Microsoft's current progress and the fundamental physics challenges that make topological qubits both incredibly difficult to realize and potentially game-changing for fault-tolerant quantum computing.

— Mark Eatherly
