Xanadu Becomes First Pure-Play Photonic Quantum Computing Company to Go Public

Source: The Quantum Insider March 27, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This milestone marks a significant moment for the quantum computing industry, as Xanadu becomes the first photonic quantum computing company to achieve a public listing, joining the ranks of other publicly traded quantum firms like IBM, IonQ, and Rigetti. The move provides crucial validation for the photonic approach to quantum computing, which offers unique advantages including room-temperature operation and natural compatibility with existing telecommunications infrastructure. Xanadu's public debut could accelerate development of their continuous-variable quantum systems and X-Series machines, while also bringing increased scrutiny and investor expectations to a company that has been making steady progress in both hardware development and quantum software platforms. This IPO represents broader maturation of the quantum sector, as specialized quantum companies gain access to public capital markets to fund their ambitious technological roadmaps.

— Mark Eatherly
