SuperQ Joins QSECDEF Alliance to Support Sovereign Post-Quantum Transition

Source: Quantum Computing Report April 08, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This alliance formation represents a critical shift toward coordinated defense against the looming quantum cryptographic threat, as organizations recognize that post-quantum security cannot be tackled in isolation. The timing is particularly significant given Google's 2029 migration deadline and the increasing urgency around protecting critical infrastructure before cryptographically relevant quantum computers emerge. SuperQ's involvement in QSECDEF demonstrates how quantum computing companies are expanding beyond hardware development to actively participate in the cybersecurity transition that their own technology necessitates. This collaboration underscores the growing maturity of the quantum industry, where companies are taking responsibility for both creating the quantum advantage and defending against its potential misuse.

— Mark Eatherly
