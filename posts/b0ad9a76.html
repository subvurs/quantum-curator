Cleveland Clinic and IBM Implement Quantum Workflow for Protein Simulation

Source: Quantum Computing Report March 26, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This collaboration marks a significant milestone in quantum-classical hybrid computing, demonstrating the first practical implementation of quantum hardware for protein electronic structure calculations using IBM's quantum-centric supercomputing approach. The successful modeling of the 303-atom Trp-cage miniprotein showcases how quantum processors can tackle the exponential scaling problem that has long plagued classical computers in molecular simulations. While still in early stages, this workflow represents a crucial step toward quantum advantage in drug discovery and materials science, where understanding protein behavior at the quantum level could revolutionize pharmaceutical research. The integration of IBM's Quantum Heron processor with classical HPC resources also validates the hybrid computing model as the most promising near-term path for quantum applications in computational chemistry.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Cleveland Clinic and IBM have reported the first simulation of a protein’s electronic structure using a quantum-centric supercomputing (QCSC) workflow. The research team modeled the 303-atom miniprotein Trp-cage using an IBM Quantum Heron r2 processor integrated with classical high-performance computing (HPC) resources.
