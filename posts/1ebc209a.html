Indian Institute of Science (IISc) and Yaqumo Inc. Sign Letter of Intent for Strategic Collaboration in Quantum Technologies

Source: The Quantum Insider May 28, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This partnership between Japan's Yaqumo and India's prestigious IISc represents a significant expansion of international quantum collaboration, particularly noteworthy as India rapidly emerges as a major player in quantum research with substantial government backing through its National Mission on Quantum Technologies. The collaboration taps into IISc's strong theoretical foundations and growing experimental capabilities while leveraging Yaqumo's quantum technology development expertise, potentially accelerating quantum innovation across both nations. Such cross-border partnerships are becoming increasingly crucial in quantum computing, where the technical challenges are so immense that pooling international talent and resources often produces breakthroughs that isolated efforts cannot achieve. This agreement also signals the maturation of quantum technology ecosystems beyond the traditional US-Europe-China triangle, with Japan-India cooperation potentially creating new pathways for quantum commercialization in Asia.

— Mark Eatherly
