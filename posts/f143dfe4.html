Helgoland 2025: the inside story of what happened on the ‘quantum island’

Source: Physics World - Quantum July 03, 2025 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article captures a historic moment as physicists gathered on the remote German island of Helgoland to commemorate the centennial of Werner Heisenberg's groundbreaking work that launched quantum mechanics in 1925. The conference brings together leading quantum researchers to reflect on how far the field has traveled from Heisenberg's original matrix mechanics to today's quantum computers, sensors, and communication systems that are reshaping technology. Beyond the symbolic importance of returning to quantum mechanics' birthplace, such gatherings offer valuable insights into where prominent physicists see the field heading next, making this both a celebration of past achievements and a roadmap for quantum's second century. The timing is particularly poignant as we stand at the threshold of practical quantum advantage in multiple applications, from cryptography to drug discovery.

— Mark Eatherly
