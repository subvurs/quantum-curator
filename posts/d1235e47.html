Tennessee Employers Explore Quantum Opportunities Through New Pre-Apprenticeship

Source: The Quantum Insider June 25, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article marks a rare convergence of workforce development and quantum technology policy, showing that regional industry groups are moving beyond research labs to build a talent pipeline for future quantum adoption. By expanding the pilot to 18 participants from diverse Tennessee firms, the Chattanooga Quantum Collaborative demonstrates how pre‑apprenticeship models can demystify quantum concepts for non‑specialists and accelerate early use‑case identification. The initiative complements recent federal funding pushes for quantum education, but its impact will hinge on whether participating companies translate the training into concrete pilot projects rather than just awareness. Readers should watch this program as a potential template for other states seeking to bridge the skills gap that currently limits commercial quantum deployment.

— Mark Eatherly
