‘We must prioritize continuity and stability to maintain momentum’: Mauro Paternostro on how to ensure that quantum tech continues to thrive

Source: Physics World - Quantum May 13, 2025 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article captures a critical moment in quantum computing's evolution, as the field transitions from pure research breakthroughs to sustained technological development requiring long-term investment strategies. Paternostro's emphasis on continuity and stability reflects growing industry awareness that quantum advantage won't emerge from sporadic funding bursts, but rather from consistent, patient capital that allows complex quantum systems to mature over multiple development cycles. His perspective is particularly timely as governments and corporations worldwide are making billion-dollar quantum commitments, yet many programs still operate on short funding cycles that can disrupt the deep, methodical work quantum technologies demand. The insights offer valuable guidance for policymakers and industry leaders navigating the delicate balance between maintaining innovation momentum and building the stable ecosystem quantum computing needs to fulfill its transformative potential.

— Mark Eatherly
