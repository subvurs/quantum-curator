Metasurfaces create super-sized neutral atom arrays for quantum computing

Source: Physics World - Quantum February 06, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This breakthrough in metasurface technology represents a crucial step toward massively scalable neutral atom quantum computers, potentially enabling systems with over 100,000 qubits compared to today's hundreds of qubits. Neutral atom platforms have emerged as strong contenders in the quantum computing race due to their identical qubits and flexible connectivity, but scaling them to useful sizes has been a major engineering challenge. The ability to create super-sized atomic arrays using metasurfaces could give neutral atom systems a significant advantage over other quantum computing approaches, as they would offer both the qubit count needed for practical quantum algorithms and the pristine control required for fault-tolerant operations. This development positions neutral atom quantum computers as serious competitors to superconducting and trapped ion systems in the quest for quantum advantage at scale.

— Mark Eatherly
