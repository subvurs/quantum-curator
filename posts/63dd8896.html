Simultaneous Estimation of Partial-Transpose Moments with Active Memory Independent of the Moment Order

Source: arXiv Quantum Physics June 12, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This research tackles a fundamental challenge in quantum state characterization by developing memory-efficient methods to simultaneously estimate multiple partial-transpose moments, which are crucial for detecting quantum entanglement and understanding bipartite quantum systems. The breakthrough lies in achieving constant memory usage (just 2n+1 qubits) regardless of how many moments are being estimated, while maintaining near-optimal sample complexity that scales only logarithmically worse than theoretical limits. This work extends beyond theoretical interest because partial-transpose moments are essential tools for entanglement detection and quantum state tomography, and the memory-efficient approach could make such characterizations practical on near-term quantum devices with limited qubit resources. The authors' demonstration that you can extract rich spectral information about quantum states without exponentially scaling memory requirements represents a significant step toward scalable quantum state analysis protocols.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

We study the simultaneous estimation of partial-transpose moments $p_j(ρ_{AB})=\mathrm{Tr}[(ρ_{AB}^{T_B})^j]$, $j=2,\ldots,K$, of an unknown bipartite $n$-qubit state from independent copies under an explicit active-memory constraint. We give a sequential qubit-reuse realization of the partial-transpose permutation that uses at most $2n+1$ active qubits, independent of $K$, and estimates all moments $p_2,\ldots,p_K$ to uniform additive error $ε$ with total copy complexity $O(K\log K/ε^2)$. We also prove two converse bounds. First, any uniformly accurate simultaneous estimator requires $Ω(K/ε^2)$ copies in the worst case. Second, the same scaling holds on an explicit isospectral two-qubit negative-partial-transpose (NPT) family whose ordinary moments are constant while the partial-transpose moments vary. These results characterize the copy complexity of the partial-transpose moment hierarchy up to a logarithmic factor and extend simultaneous nonlinear-functional estimation from ordinary state powers to partial-transpose spectral data under active quantum memory independent of the target moment order.
