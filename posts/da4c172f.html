New Investment in Quantum Motion Strengthens Europe’s Push for Scalable Quantum Computing

Source: The Quantum Insider May 07, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This substantial $160 million Series C funding for Quantum Motion represents a significant vote of confidence in Europe's quantum computing ambitions, particularly as the company pursues silicon-based quantum processors that could leverage existing semiconductor manufacturing infrastructure. The investment from Firgun Ventures marks their first European quantum bet, suggesting growing international recognition that London and the broader European quantum ecosystem are becoming serious competitors to US and Chinese quantum efforts. Quantum Motion's approach of building quantum chips using conventional silicon fabrication techniques could prove crucial for eventual scalability, as it avoids the exotic materials and extreme cooling requirements that plague many current quantum computing approaches. This funding round positions Europe to play a more prominent role in the global race toward practical, fault-tolerant quantum computers.

— Mark Eatherly
