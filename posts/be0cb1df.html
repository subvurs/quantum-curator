John Martinis, Nobel Prize–Winning Physicist, Joins White House Science And Technology Panel

Source: The Quantum Insider March 26, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This appointment represents a significant boost for quantum computing's influence in federal policy, as Martinis brings deep technical expertise from his pioneering work on superconducting qubits and his leadership role in achieving Google's quantum supremacy milestone. Having a Nobel laureate with hands-on quantum hardware experience advising the White House could accelerate national quantum initiatives and help ensure America's quantum policies are grounded in cutting-edge scientific understanding. The timing is particularly crucial as the U.S. faces intensifying global competition in quantum technologies, where strategic government support and coordination between academia, industry, and federal agencies will be essential for maintaining technological leadership. This article signals that quantum computing continues to rise in national security and economic priority discussions at the highest levels of government.

— Mark Eatherly
