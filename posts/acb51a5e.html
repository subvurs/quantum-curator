IonQ and University of Maryland Expand QLab Partnership to Advance Quantum Networking

Source: Quantum Computing Report April 14, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This partnership expansion represents a significant investment in quantum networking infrastructure, an area that's become increasingly critical as the field moves beyond isolated quantum computers toward interconnected quantum systems. The collaboration between IonQ and University of Maryland through the National Quantum Laboratory positions Maryland as a major hub for quantum research, particularly important given UMD's strong track record in trapped-ion quantum computing and networking research. The $7.5 million commitment suggests serious momentum behind practical quantum networking applications, which could eventually enable distributed quantum computing and ultra-secure quantum communications networks. This type of industry-academia partnership is exactly what's needed to bridge the gap between quantum computing research breakthroughs and real-world quantum network deployments.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

IonQ (NYSE: IONQ) and the University of Maryland (UMD) have announced a $7.5 million expansion of their partnership through the National Quantum Laboratory (QLab). Supported by the State of Maryland’s Capital of Quantum Initiative, this multi-year agreement builds upon a strategic collaboration first established in 2024.
