International scientists head into the fast-lane of Denmark’s burgeoning quantum ecosystem

Source: Physics World - Quantum March 17, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

Denmark's quantum ecosystem is rapidly emerging as a major European hub, attracting top international talent through strategic investments and collaborative research initiatives. This development signals a significant shift in the global quantum landscape, where smaller nations with focused strategies can compete effectively against larger quantum programs by leveraging their strengths in fundamental research and fostering tight-knit innovation networks. The influx of international scientists suggests Denmark is successfully creating the kind of collaborative environment that has proven essential for quantum breakthroughs, positioning itself as a key player in Europe's broader quantum sovereignty efforts. This trend reflects how quantum computing success increasingly depends not just on funding scale, but on creating the right ecosystem conditions for interdisciplinary collaboration and talent retention.

— Mark Eatherly
