Research Team Finds Useful Quantum Computers Could Be Built with as Few as 10,000 Qubits

Source: The Quantum Insider March 31, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This research represents a potentially game-changing shift in quantum computing scalability, as previous estimates suggested that fault-tolerant quantum computers would need millions of physical qubits to perform useful calculations. The collaboration between Caltech theorists and Oratomic demonstrates how academic-industry partnerships are driving practical breakthroughs in error correction, one of quantum computing's most fundamental challenges. If validated, this 10,000-qubit threshold could dramatically accelerate the timeline for commercially viable quantum computers, bringing applications like drug discovery and cryptography breaking from decades away to potentially within the next 10-15 years. The work highlights how clever algorithmic and hardware innovations can sometimes overcome what seemed like insurmountable physical limitations in quantum systems.

— Mark Eatherly
