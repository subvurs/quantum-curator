SEEQC Awarded Participation in U.S. CHIPS Act–Backed Microelectronics Commons NORDTECH Quantum R&D Program

Source: The Quantum Insider June 16, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

SEEQC's inclusion in the CHIPS Act-backed NORDTECH program represents a significant federal endorsement of their energy-efficient quantum chip approach and signals growing government recognition that quantum computing infrastructure needs dedicated semiconductor manufacturing pathways. This four-year funding through the Microelectronics Commons hub system positions SEEQC to accelerate development of their cryogenic classical-quantum hybrid chips, which aim to solve one of quantum computing's thorniest problems: bringing control electronics closer to qubits without adding excess heat. The regional hub model is particularly strategic here, as it could help establish specialized quantum semiconductor fabrication capabilities in the Northeast, potentially reducing dependence on general-purpose fabs that aren't optimized for quantum applications. For an industry still finding its manufacturing footing, this kind of dedicated infrastructure investment could prove as important as the qubit breakthroughs that tend to grab headlines.

— Mark Eatherly
