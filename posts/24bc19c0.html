Quantum physicists Charles Bennett and Gilles Brassard win $1m Turing Award

Source: Physics World - Quantum March 18, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This recognition marks a historic moment where quantum cryptography pioneers Charles Bennett and Gilles Brassard receive computing's highest honor, highlighting how quantum physics has fundamentally transformed information security. Their groundbreaking BB84 protocol, developed in 1984, established the theoretical foundation for quantum key distribution and proved that the laws of quantum mechanics could guarantee absolutely secure communication. The Turing Award acknowledgment signals that quantum information science has matured from exotic physics experiment to a cornerstone technology that major computing institutions recognize as revolutionary. This award validates quantum cryptography as not just a niche scientific pursuit, but as a field that will reshape how we think about digital security in an era of advancing quantum computers.

— Mark Eatherly
