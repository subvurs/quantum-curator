The Conversation: Quantum Computers Could Expose Our Digital Secrets – But there are Much Better Reasons to Build them

Source: The Quantum Insider June 02, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article tackles one of the most persistent misconceptions about quantum computing - that breaking encryption is the primary or only reason to develop these machines. While quantum computers will indeed pose challenges to current cryptographic systems, this piece from Royal Holloway researchers emphasizes the far more exciting potential applications, from drug discovery and materials science to optimization problems that could revolutionize logistics and finance. The timing is particularly relevant as we're seeing increased investment in quantum-safe cryptography alongside rapid progress in quantum hardware, making it crucial for the public to understand that quantum computing's true promise extends far beyond cybersecurity concerns. This balanced perspective helps counter the often sensationalized "quantum apocalypse" narrative while highlighting why quantum computing represents one of the most transformative technologies of our time.

— Mark Eatherly
