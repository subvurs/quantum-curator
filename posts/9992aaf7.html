Cavity-Free Distributed Quantum Computing with Rydberg Ensembles via Collective Enhancement

Source: arXiv Quantum Physics March 16, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article presents a breakthrough approach to quantum networking that could dramatically simplify the hardware requirements for distributed quantum computing by eliminating the need for optical cavities—typically among the most challenging and expensive components in quantum systems. By leveraging collective enhancement in Rydberg atom ensembles, the researchers achieve impressive performance metrics including 99.93% gate fidelity and 600 Hz entanglement generation rates at 20 km distances, proving that cavity-free architectures can compete with traditional cavity-based approaches. The practical significance is enormous: removing optical cavities makes quantum networks far more accessible and scalable, potentially accelerating the development of multi-node quantum computers and quantum internet infrastructure. This work represents a crucial step toward making distributed quantum computing systems more practical and cost-effective for real-world deployment.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

A complete architecture for cavity-free quantum networking based on collective enhancement in Rydberg atom ensembles is presented. The protocol exploits Rydberg blockade and phase-matched directional emission to eliminate optical cavities without sacrificing performance. The architecture comprises three steps: (i) local control-ensemble entanglement via Rydberg blockade with fidelity $F_{\mathrm{gate}} \approx 99.93\%$; (ii) atom-photon conversion via Raman transitions, achieving directional emission ($η_{\mathrm{dir}} \approx 35\%$) and single-node efficiency $η_{\mathrm{node}} \approx 19\%$; and (iii) remote atom-atom entanglement via Hong-Ou-Mandel interference, producing Bell states with fidelity $F > 97.5\%$. With quantum memories enabling retry protocols, entanglement generation rates exceed $600$ Hz at 20 km separation. This cavity-free approach provides a practical and scalable pathway for distributed quantum computing and secure quantum communication.
