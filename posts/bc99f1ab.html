Horizon Quantum Selects Dublin for Second Quantum Computer Testbed

Source: The Quantum Insider June 11, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This development signals Horizon Quantum's ambitious push to establish Europe as a major hub for quantum computing infrastructure, with Dublin emerging as a strategic location that could rival established quantum centers in the US and Asia. The deployment of an IonQ 256-qubit trapped ion system represents a significant leap in accessible quantum computing power for European researchers and enterprises, potentially accelerating quantum software development and applications across the continent. What's particularly noteworthy is Horizon Quantum's focus on software infrastructure rather than hardware development, positioning them to bridge the critical gap between quantum hardware capabilities and practical applications that businesses can actually use. This move could catalyze a new wave of quantum innovation in Europe by providing local access to cutting-edge quantum systems without the complexities of international data transfer restrictions.

— Mark Eatherly
