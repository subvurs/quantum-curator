QuantumDiamonds Deploys First Asian Quantum Sensing System QD m.1 at Taiwan’s iST

Source: Quantum Computing Report May 03, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This deployment marks a significant milestone in quantum sensing commercialization, demonstrating that quantum technologies are moving beyond research labs into real-world semiconductor manufacturing and failure analysis applications. Taiwan's position as a global semiconductor hub makes this installation particularly strategic, as quantum sensing systems like the QDm.1 can provide unprecedented precision in detecting defects and analyzing chip performance at the nanoscale. The expansion of QuantumDiamonds into Asia follows growing industry recognition that quantum sensors offer practical advantages today, even as quantum computers continue development toward fault-tolerance. This represents the kind of near-term quantum technology deployment that could drive substantial commercial value while the field develops more ambitious quantum computing capabilities.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

QuantumDiamonds GmbH, a Munich-based specialist in quantum sensing for semiconductor testing, has successfully deployed its QDm.1 system at the Integrated Service Technology (iST) facility in Hsinchu, Taiwan. This installation represents the company’s first commercial footprint in Asia and follows its recent expansion into the United States.
