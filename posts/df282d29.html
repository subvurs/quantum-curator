Above 99.9% Fidelity Single-Qubit Gates, Two-Qubit Gates, and Readout in a Single Superconducting Quantum Device

Source: PRX Quantum May 20, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This remarkable achievement represents a crucial milestone for superconducting quantum computers, as maintaining above 99.9% fidelity across all fundamental operations simultaneously has been one of the field's most persistent challenges. The breakthrough demonstrates that careful engineering of qubit-coupler interactions can overcome the typical trade-offs between gate speed, coherence, and crosstalk that have plagued scalable quantum systems. This level of precision brings superconducting qubits tantalizingly close to the error correction threshold needed for fault-tolerant quantum computing, potentially accelerating the timeline for practical quantum algorithms. The work's emphasis on simultaneous optimization of all quantum operations marks a significant step toward the unified, high-fidelity control required for large-scale quantum processors.

— Mark Eatherly

Author(s): Fabian Marxer et al.
