Global Firms Announce Plans for Southeastern Quantum Campus

Source: The Quantum Insider April 15, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This announcement signals a significant shift toward establishing dedicated quantum infrastructure hubs outside traditional tech corridors, potentially democratizing access to quantum resources across broader geographic regions. The southeastern United States has been emerging as an attractive destination for quantum investments due to lower operational costs, growing university partnerships, and state-level incentives, making this campus development a strategic move that could accelerate regional quantum ecosystem growth. By bringing together research institutions, workforce development, and industry partners in a centralized facility, this initiative addresses one of quantum computing's key challenges: the need for concentrated expertise and specialized infrastructure to bridge the gap between laboratory breakthroughs and commercial applications. The collaboration between JLL's real estate expertise and Qlocity's quantum commercialization focus suggests a maturing industry that's moving beyond pure research toward practical deployment considerations.

— Mark Eatherly
