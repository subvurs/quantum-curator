Modelling wavefunction collapse as a continuous flow yields insights on the nature of measurement

Source: Physics World - Quantum January 22, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article explores a fascinating theoretical framework that treats quantum measurement not as an instantaneous collapse but as a continuous flowing process, offering new insights into one of quantum mechanics' most puzzling aspects. By modeling wavefunction collapse through quantum state diffusion and connecting it to entropy production, researchers are developing a more nuanced understanding of how quantum information behaves during measurement - a fundamental process that underlies all quantum sensing and computing applications. This work could prove particularly valuable for quantum sensing technologies, where understanding the precise dynamics of measurement processes is crucial for optimizing detector sensitivity and minimizing decoherence effects. While still theoretical, this approach represents an intriguing step toward demystifying the measurement problem that has puzzled physicists since the early days of quantum mechanics.

— Mark Eatherly
