Aliro, zerothird, and Cisco Demonstrate Operational Entanglement-Based QKD on Enterprise Routers

Source: Quantum Computing Report July 14, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

AI Commentary

This article marks the first time an entanglement‑based QKD system has been integrated and managed in real time on commercial‑grade enterprise routers, showing that quantum‑secure links can move beyond laboratory testbeds into existing network infrastructure. By combining Aliro’s orchestration software with zerothird’s photon sources and Cisco’s routing hardware, the demo bridges the gap between quantum networking research and practical deployment, echoing recent progress in satellite QKD and fiber‑based key distribution. The ability to automate monitoring and security controls on production equipment suggests a viable path for enterprises to adopt quantum‑safe communications without wholesale hardware replacement, though scaling the entangled photon rates and maintaining low error rates over longer distances remain challenges to be solved.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Quantum networking software provider Aliro Technologies and Austrian quantum scale-up zerothird have announced a joint quantum-safe networking deployment with Cisco. Conducted at the Cisco Photonics Center in Vimercate, Italy, the project demonstrates real-time orchestration, monitoring, and automated security controls for a live, entanglement-based quantum key distribution (eQKD) network running directly on production-grade routing hardware.
