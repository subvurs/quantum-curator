D-Wave Quantum Rides a New ‘Wave’ of Quantum Bookings, As Demand — and Volatility — Increase in Quantum

Source: The Quantum Insider May 12, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This dramatic surge in D-Wave's bookings - nearly 2,000% year-over-year growth - signals that quantum annealing applications are finally gaining serious commercial traction, even as the broader quantum industry grapples with the typical volatility of emerging technologies. The disconnect between soaring bookings and declining revenue highlights the complex dynamics of quantum commercialization, where organizations are increasingly committing to quantum projects but deployments often lag due to integration challenges and the experimental nature of many applications. D-Wave's quantum annealing approach, which targets optimization problems rather than universal quantum computing, appears to be resonating with enterprises looking for near-term quantum advantages in logistics, finance, and machine learning. This booking momentum could be an early indicator that the quantum industry is transitioning from pure research curiosity to practical business tool, though the revenue lag reminds us that turning quantum potential into quantum profits remains a work in progress.

— Mark Eatherly
