Dual-rail superconducting qubits generate high-fidelity logical entanglement, study finds

Source: Phys.org Quantum March 26, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This study represents a significant step toward practical quantum error correction, as dual-rail encoding offers a promising path to creating more stable logical qubits from noisy physical ones. By achieving high-fidelity logical entanglement between these encoded qubits, researchers are demonstrating that we can maintain quantum coherence even while adding the redundancy needed for error correction. This approach is particularly exciting for superconducting quantum systems because it could help bridge the gap between today's noisy intermediate-scale quantum devices and the fault-tolerant quantum computers needed for breakthrough applications. The ability to generate reliable logical entanglement is a crucial building block for scaling up quantum computers while keeping error rates manageable.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Quantum computers, systems that process information leveraging quantum mechanical effects, could outperform classical computers on some advanced tasks. These systems rely on qubits, the fundamental units of quantum information, that become linked via an effect known as quantum entanglement and share a unified quantum state.
