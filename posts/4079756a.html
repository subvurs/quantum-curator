IonQ Selected for DARPA’s Heterogeneous Architectures for Quantum (HARQ) Program

Source: The Quantum Insider April 14, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This DARPA contract positions IonQ at the forefront of one of quantum computing's most crucial next frontiers: building heterogeneous quantum networks that can link different types of quantum processors together. The HARQ program represents a significant shift from today's isolated quantum computers toward interconnected quantum systems that could dramatically expand computational capabilities by leveraging the strengths of different quantum technologies simultaneously. IonQ's trapped-ion technology, known for its high-fidelity operations and connectivity, makes it an ideal candidate for quantum networking applications where maintaining quantum coherence across distributed systems is paramount. This development signals growing military and government recognition that the future of quantum advantage likely lies not in single monolithic machines, but in sophisticated quantum networks that can tackle problems no individual quantum computer could handle alone.

— Mark Eatherly
