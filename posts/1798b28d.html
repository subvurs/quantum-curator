Osaka University and Fixstars Break 40-Qubit Barrier in Quantum Chemistry Simulation

Source: Quantum Computing Report April 05, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This breakthrough represents a significant milestone in classical quantum simulation, with Osaka University and Fixstars successfully modeling quantum chemistry problems at the 40-qubit scale using advanced classical computing techniques. While this doesn't involve actual quantum hardware, classical simulation at this scale is crucial for validating quantum algorithms and understanding the computational complexity boundaries where quantum computers will eventually demonstrate clear advantages. The achievement provides valuable benchmarks for near-term quantum devices and helps researchers better understand how quantum chemistry simulations scale with system size, particularly for drug discovery and materials science applications. This work also highlights the ongoing "quantum simulation race" where classical computers continue pushing their limits, ultimately defining more precisely where quantum supremacy will emerge in practical applications.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Large-scale classical simulation of IQPE quantum circuits demonstrated in this work. Larger qubit counts and more Hamiltonian terms result in deeper circuits and longer simulation times.
