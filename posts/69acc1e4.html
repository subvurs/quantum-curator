Osaka University and Fixstars Break 40-Qubit Barrier in Quantum Chemistry Simulation

Source: Quantum Computing Report April 05, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This breakthrough demonstrates the impressive power of classical simulation techniques, with Osaka University and Fixstars pushing quantum chemistry simulations to 40 qubits using sophisticated classical computing methods. While this achievement highlights how classical computers can still tackle certain quantum problems at meaningful scales, it also serves as a crucial benchmark for quantum advantage research - helping define exactly where quantum computers need to perform to claim superiority over classical approaches. The work is particularly significant for quantum chemistry applications, where understanding molecular behavior at larger scales could accelerate drug discovery and materials science, regardless of whether the simulation runs on classical or quantum hardware. This classical milestone essentially raises the bar for quantum computers, making the eventual demonstration of quantum advantage in chemistry simulations even more meaningful when it occurs.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Large-scale classical simulation of IQPE quantum circuits demonstrated in this work. Larger qubit counts and more Hamiltonian terms result in deeper circuits and longer simulation times.
