Error mitigation on Amazon Braket with program sets and Mitiq

Source: AWS Quantum Computing Blog March 23, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article highlights Amazon's integration of Mitiq, an open-source quantum error mitigation library, with their Braket cloud platform, making advanced noise reduction techniques more accessible to quantum developers. Error mitigation represents a crucial bridge technology for the current NISQ (noisy intermediate-scale quantum) era, allowing researchers to extract meaningful results from today's imperfect quantum processors without waiting for full fault tolerance. The collaboration between AWS and Unitary Fund's Mitiq project demonstrates how cloud providers are recognizing that sophisticated error handling tools are essential for practical quantum computing applications. This integration could significantly lower the barrier for quantum researchers to implement proven mitigation strategies like zero-noise extrapolation and probabilistic error cancellation in their experiments.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

To get the most out of today’s noisy quantum computers, quantum computational experiments commonly utilize quantum error mitigation (QEM) techniques. These methods include discarding known noisy results (post selection), modifying underlying pulse sequences, amplifying noise within a quantum circuit, and many more.
