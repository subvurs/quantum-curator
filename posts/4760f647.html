IBM, Academic Partners Use Quantum Computer to Reproduce Key Material Properties, Testing Early Scientific Usefulness

Source: The Quantum Insider March 26, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This collaboration represents a significant milestone in demonstrating that current noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) devices can tackle real scientific problems, not just theoretical demonstrations. By successfully reproducing experimentally measured magnetic material properties, the team has shown that quantum computers may contribute to materials science research even before we achieve full quantum error correction - a timeline that could still be years away. This finding is particularly important because it suggests the quantum advantage for certain scientific applications might arrive sooner than expected, potentially accelerating discoveries in areas like drug development, catalyst design, and energy storage materials. The work validates the growing consensus that NISQ-era quantum computers, despite their limitations, can provide genuine scientific value when applied to carefully selected problems that align with their current capabilities.

— Mark Eatherly
