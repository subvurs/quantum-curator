Single metasurface could generate record numbers of trapped neutral atoms

Source: Physics World - Quantum March 16, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This breakthrough in metasurface technology could dramatically accelerate the path to large-scale neutral atom quantum computers by potentially trapping over 100,000 atoms on a single device. Neutral atom systems are emerging as serious contenders for fault-tolerant quantum computing because they offer excellent qubit connectivity and can be precisely controlled with optical tweezers, but scaling to the massive qubit counts needed for practical applications has remained a major engineering challenge. The ability to generate such unprecedented numbers of trapped atoms from a single metasurface represents a significant step toward the million-qubit systems that many experts believe will be necessary for solving real-world problems like drug discovery and materials science. This development positions neutral atom platforms to compete more directly with superconducting and trapped ion approaches in the race to build commercially viable quantum computers.

— Mark Eatherly
