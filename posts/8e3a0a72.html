At Its First NYSE Investor Day, D-Wave Bets on Two Quantum Machines, Not One

Source: The Quantum Insider June 02, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article highlights D-Wave's strategic pivot toward a dual-technology approach, combining their established quantum annealing systems with gate-based quantum computers to address different problem types. D-Wave's CEO taking the unusual step of encouraging investor skepticism demonstrates remarkable transparency in an industry often criticized for overhyped promises, potentially setting a new standard for how quantum companies communicate with stakeholders. The timing of this investor day at the NYSE signals D-Wave's confidence in their commercial viability as they position themselves to compete across multiple quantum computing paradigms. This dual-machine strategy could prove crucial as the quantum industry matures and different applications require specialized quantum architectures.

— Mark Eatherly
