Colt and Ciena Complete Quantum-Safe Transatlantic Network Trial

Source: The Quantum Insider June 10, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This transatlantic quantum-safe network trial represents a crucial milestone in preparing our global internet infrastructure for the post-quantum era, demonstrating that quantum-resistant encryption can operate at scale across real-world distances without sacrificing performance. The 6900km route proves that quantum-safe protocols can handle the latency and signal degradation challenges of intercontinental fiber networks, addressing one of the key practical concerns about deploying post-quantum cryptography globally. While quantum computers capable of breaking current encryption are still years away, this trial shows the telecommunications industry is proactively building the secure infrastructure we'll need, rather than waiting for a quantum crisis to force hasty upgrades. The achievement marks an important step toward quantum-safe communications becoming standard practice rather than experimental technology.

— Mark Eatherly
