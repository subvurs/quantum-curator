Qiskit-Braket provider v0.11: New Primitives and Flexible Circuit Compilation

Source: AWS Quantum Computing Blog February 20, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This update represents a significant step toward quantum software interoperability, allowing developers to seamlessly use Qiskit's powerful development tools while accessing Amazon Braket's diverse hardware backends. The addition of flexible circuit compilation options is particularly noteworthy, as it gives researchers more control over how their quantum algorithms are optimized for different hardware platforms, potentially improving performance across IBM, IonQ, Rigetti, and other quantum processors available through Braket. This kind of cross-platform compatibility is crucial for the quantum computing ecosystem's maturation, enabling developers to write once and deploy across multiple quantum cloud services without being locked into a single vendor's toolchain.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

We recently released v0.11 of the Qiskit-Braket provider, which brings more Qiskit features to Amazon Braket users, improves access to Braket backends through Qiskit, and also enables compilation on the Braket SDK or with OpenQASM Programs.
