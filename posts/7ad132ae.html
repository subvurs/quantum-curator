SEALSQ and WISeKey Outline Strategic Progress on Space-Based Quantum Spatial Orbital Cloud

Source: Quantum Computing Report June 13, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article highlights an ambitious intersection of quantum cryptography and space technology, as SEALSQ and WISeKey advance their plans for deploying post-quantum security infrastructure directly in orbit. The Quantum Spatial Orbital Cloud concept represents a fascinating approach to addressing quantum-safe communications by placing tamper-resistant cryptographic nodes beyond terrestrial interference, potentially offering unprecedented security for global communications networks. While space-based quantum infrastructure remains in early development stages, this initiative reflects growing recognition that the quantum threat to current encryption requires innovative deployment strategies that extend beyond traditional ground-based solutions. The combination of certified quantum randomness generation and post-quantum cryptography in LEO satellites could eventually provide a robust foundation for securing critical communications against both classical and quantum computational attacks.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Post-quantum semiconductor developer SEALSQ Corp and its parent company WISeKey International Holding Ltd have detailed engineering and deployment milestones for their Quantum Spatial Orbital Cloud (QSOC) platform.
