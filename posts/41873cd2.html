Could silicon become the bedrock of quantum computers?

Source: Physics World - Quantum January 26, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This development from Silicon Quantum Computing represents a significant milestone in the quest to build quantum computers using silicon, the same material that powers today's classical computers. While 11 qubits may seem modest compared to superconducting systems with hundreds of qubits, silicon-based quantum processors offer compelling advantages including potentially better scalability, longer coherence times, and the ability to leverage existing semiconductor manufacturing infrastructure. The fact that this Australian spin-out has achieved what they claim is "modality-leading" performance suggests silicon quantum computing is maturing from a promising research direction into a viable commercial path. If silicon qubits can maintain their advantages while scaling up, this could dramatically accelerate quantum computing adoption by tapping into the trillion-dollar semiconductor industry's established supply chains and expertise.

— Mark Eatherly
