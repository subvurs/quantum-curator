Researchers Demonstrate Stable Links For Quantum Networks Over Kilometers of Noisy Fiber

Source: The Quantum Insider April 02, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This demonstration represents a crucial milestone in the practical deployment of quantum networks, as maintaining quantum coherence over long distances through real-world fiber infrastructure has been one of the biggest engineering challenges facing the field. The ability to preserve quantum properties across kilometers of "noisy" fiber - the kind actually deployed in telecommunications networks rather than pristine laboratory conditions - brings us significantly closer to a functional quantum internet that could connect quantum computers and enable ultra-secure communications. This work addresses the gap between laboratory quantum networking experiments and the messy reality of existing fiber infrastructure, potentially accelerating the timeline for quantum network deployment by leveraging infrastructure that's already in the ground. The stabilization techniques developed here could become foundational for the first generation of metropolitan-scale quantum networks.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Insider Brief PRESS RELEASE — Researchers have shown that a single photon carrying quantum information can travel down kilometers of noisy real-world fiber and arrive at the far end with their quantum properties largely preserved.
