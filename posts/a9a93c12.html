EPB Joins Southeastern Quantum Collaborative to Support Regional Infrastructure Integration

Source: Quantum Computing Report April 01, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This development highlights how quantum computing is transitioning from isolated research efforts to coordinated regional ecosystems that bring together universities, major quantum companies like IBM and IonQ, and critical infrastructure providers. EPB's participation is particularly noteworthy given their expertise in advanced networking infrastructure, which will be essential for connecting quantum systems across the Southeast and potentially enabling distributed quantum computing architectures. The Southeastern Quantum Collaborative represents a strategic approach to building the foundational infrastructure and workforce needed to compete in the quantum race, following successful models like the Chicago Quantum Network. This type of regional coordination could accelerate practical quantum deployments by ensuring the necessary telecommunications backbone and skilled workforce are developed in parallel with the quantum hardware itself.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

EPB has joined the Southeastern Quantum Collaborative (SQC) as an inaugural member. Led by the University of Alabama in Huntsville (UAH), the SQC is a regional association of academic, industrial, and government organizations—including IBM, D-Wave, and IonQ—tasked with coordinating quantum information science research and workforce development across the Southeast.
