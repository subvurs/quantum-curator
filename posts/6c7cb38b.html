U.S. Department of Energy Issues RFI for 2028 Fault-Tolerant Quantum Computer

Source: Quantum Computing Report May 15, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This DOE request marks a pivotal moment in quantum computing as the U.S. government formally commits to deploying fault-tolerant quantum systems within just four years—an aggressive timeline that reflects growing confidence in the technology's maturation. The initiative signals a shift from research to practical implementation, positioning America's national laboratories as testbeds for quantum advantage in scientific computing applications like materials science and drug discovery. While achieving true fault tolerance by 2028 remains technically challenging, this government backing could accelerate private sector development and help determine which quantum architectures are ready for real-world deployment. The RFI essentially puts the quantum industry on notice that the era of small-scale demonstrations is ending and the race for commercially viable quantum computing has officially begun.

— Mark Eatherly
