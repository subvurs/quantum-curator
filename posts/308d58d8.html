Finland Launches QScale Project to Advance Quantum Computing Scalability

Source: The Quantum Insider June 02, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This Finnish initiative represents a crucial step toward solving one of quantum computing's most pressing challenges - scaling up while maintaining energy efficiency and signal precision. The QScale project's focus on optical control and ultra-precise signal technology addresses the fundamental bottleneck that has limited quantum computers to relatively small qubit counts, as traditional electronic control systems become increasingly complex and error-prone at scale. With Finland's strong track record in quantum research and VTT's expertise in photonics, this collaboration could yield breakthrough approaches that make fault-tolerant quantum computers with thousands of qubits more feasible. The project's emphasis on energy efficiency is particularly timely, as the quantum computing field grapples with the environmental impact of scaling up these inherently power-hungry systems.

— Mark Eatherly
