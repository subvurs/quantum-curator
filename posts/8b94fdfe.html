Origin Quantum Unveils Origin Wukong-180 Fourth-Generation Quantum Computer

Source: Quantum Computing Report May 14, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This marks a significant milestone for China's quantum computing ambitions, as Origin Quantum becomes one of the few companies globally to reach a fourth-generation superconducting quantum processor while successfully commercializing their technology through cloud access. The rapid progression from their third-generation system in 2024 to the Wukong-180 demonstrates the accelerating pace of development in China's quantum sector, which has been heavily backed by government investment. The integration into a global cloud platform is particularly noteworthy, as it signals China's intent to compete directly with established quantum cloud services from IBM, Google, and other Western companies in the international market. This development adds another major player to the intensifying global race for quantum advantage, potentially reshaping the competitive landscape as quantum systems become more accessible worldwide.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Origin Quantum officially launched the Origin Wukong-180, marking the fourth generation of its domestically developed superconducting quantum computing line. Headquartered in Hefei, China, the company has integrated the system into its global cloud platform to process external computational tasks.
