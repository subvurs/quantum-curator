Fujitsu, The University of Osaka Develop New Technologies For Chemical Material Energy Calculations on Early Quantum Computers

Source: The Quantum Insider March 25, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This collaboration between Fujitsu and the University of Osaka represents a pragmatic approach to bridging today's noisy quantum computers with tomorrow's fault-tolerant systems, focusing specifically on chemical material energy calculations that could revolutionize drug discovery and materials science. The development of their enhanced STAR architecture version 3 is particularly significant because it addresses one of quantum computing's most promising near-term applications while acknowledging the current limitations of available hardware. Chemical simulations have long been considered quantum computing's "killer app" since molecules are inherently quantum mechanical systems, making this work a crucial stepping stone toward practical quantum advantage in pharmaceutical and materials research. The timing is especially relevant as multiple companies race to demonstrate quantum utility in chemistry applications, with this approach potentially offering a more accessible path for researchers working with early fault-tolerant quantum computers.

— Mark Eatherly
