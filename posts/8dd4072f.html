Pasqal Inaugurates Italy’s First Neutral-Atom Quantum Computer at CINECA Supercomputing Center

Source: Quantum Computing Report June 12, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This milestone represents a significant step forward for Europe's quantum computing infrastructure, as Pasqal's SOL system brings cutting-edge neutral-atom quantum technology to Italy's premier research facilities for the first time. The deployment at CINECA's supercomputing center is particularly strategic, as it positions researchers to explore hybrid classical-quantum algorithms by integrating quantum processors with traditional high-performance computing resources. Neutral-atom platforms like Pasqal's offer unique advantages including flexible qubit connectivity and the ability to reconfigure atomic arrays dynamically, making this installation valuable for both fundamental research and practical algorithm development. The partnership with Italy's national research consortium signals growing European investment in quantum computing sovereignty and could accelerate quantum research across multiple disciplines within the region.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Neutral-atom quantum hardware developer Pasqal has officially inaugurated its SOL quantum computer at the DAMA Technopole in Emilia-Romagna, Bologna.
