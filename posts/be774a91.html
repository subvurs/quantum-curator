Quobly Closes €115 Million ($133.5 Million USD) Series A to Industrialize Silicon-Spin Qubit Processors

Source: Quantum Computing Report June 03, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This funding milestone represents a significant vote of confidence in silicon-spin qubit technology, which promises superior manufacturability compared to more exotic quantum computing approaches by leveraging existing semiconductor fabrication infrastructure. Quobly's substantial Series A round, particularly with backing from France's strategic Deep Tech 2030 initiative and cybersecurity firm SEALSQ, signals Europe's commitment to building domestic quantum capabilities that could challenge the current dominance of superconducting and trapped-ion platforms. The involvement of a post-quantum cryptography specialist as a co-lead investor is particularly telling, suggesting the funding is positioned not just for quantum computing development but for the broader quantum-safe transition that organizations worldwide are beginning to navigate. With silicon-spin qubits potentially offering easier scaling pathways due to their compatibility with conventional chip manufacturing, Quobly's industrialization push could accelerate the timeline for practical quantum advantage in real-world applications.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Grenoble-based hardware venture Quobly has finalized a €115 million ($133.5 million USD) Series A financing round to transition its silicon-based quantum computing architecture from technology validation into active industrial execution.
