Universal Logical Operations Implemented in Silicon Donor Quantum Processor

Source: Quantum Computing Report March 28, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This breakthrough represents a significant step toward fault-tolerant quantum computing by demonstrating that silicon donor qubits can perform the complete set of logical operations needed for universal quantum computation. The use of isotopically purified silicon-28 with precisely positioned phosphorus donors showcases the remarkable level of atomic-scale control now possible, potentially offering a path to scalable quantum processors that could leverage existing semiconductor manufacturing infrastructure. What makes this particularly exciting is the combination of silicon's natural compatibility with classical electronics and the exceptional coherence properties of nuclear spin qubits, which could address one of quantum computing's biggest challenges: maintaining quantum information long enough to perform meaningful calculations. While still early-stage research with just five qubits, this work validates silicon donors as a promising platform that could eventually bridge the gap between laboratory demonstrations and practical quantum computers.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Researchers from the Shenzhen International Quantum Academy have demonstrated a silicon-based quantum processor capable of executing a universal set of logical gate operations. The device utilizes a cluster of five phosphorus donor nuclear spins embedded in an isotopically purified silicon-28 lattice, patterned with atomic precision via scanning tunneling microscopy (STM) lithography.
