How Noise Limits The Size of Quantum Circuits

Source: The Quantum Insider April 06, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article tackles one of the most fundamental challenges facing quantum computing today: how environmental noise places hard limits on the depth and complexity of quantum circuits we can reliably execute. The domino chain analogy effectively illustrates why even small errors compound exponentially as quantum circuits grow longer, making error correction and noise mitigation absolutely critical for scaling to practical quantum algorithms. Understanding these noise-induced limitations is essential for quantum hardware developers working to push beyond today's noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) devices toward fault-tolerant quantum computers. This represents the core engineering challenge that will determine whether quantum computing can transition from promising laboratory demonstrations to transformative real-world applications.

— Mark Eatherly
