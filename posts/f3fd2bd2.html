Google Shortens Timeline for Quantum-Safe Encryption Transition

Source: The Quantum Insider March 25, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This accelerated timeline from Google represents a significant shift in how seriously major tech companies are taking the quantum threat to current encryption standards. By moving up their post-quantum cryptography transition to 2029, Google is essentially signaling that they believe cryptographically relevant quantum computers could arrive sooner than many previous estimates suggested. The tech giant's urgency is particularly noteworthy given their own quantum computing achievements with systems like Sycamore and Willow, providing them with insider perspective on the technology's rapid advancement. This move will likely pressure other organizations to expedite their own quantum-safe transitions, as Google's infrastructure underpins countless online services that rely on secure encryption.

— Mark Eatherly
