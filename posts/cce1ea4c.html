Realizing the fermionic Laughlin state on a trapped-ion quantum processor

Source: AWS Quantum Computing Blog June 09, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This research represents a significant milestone in quantum simulation, as the fermionic Laughlin state is a highly exotic quantum many-body state that's notoriously difficult to create and study in traditional laboratory settings. The successful realization of this state on a trapped-ion quantum processor demonstrates the growing maturity of quantum hardware for tackling fundamental physics problems that are computationally intractable for classical computers. What makes this particularly exciting is that Laughlin states are connected to fractional quantum Hall effects and topological quantum computing concepts, suggesting that near-term quantum devices may soon be able to explore entirely new regimes of quantum matter. This collaboration between University of Washington researchers and Amazon's quantum team also showcases how cloud-based quantum processors are becoming powerful tools for cutting-edge scientific discovery.

— Mark Eatherly
