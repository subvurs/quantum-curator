Physicists create new family of Schrödinger-cat states

Source: Phys.org Quantum June 08, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This breakthrough expands the quantum toolkit by developing new types of superposition states that could enhance the sensitivity of quantum sensors used in gravitational wave detection, magnetic field measurements, and atomic clocks. While Schrödinger cat states have been created before, this new "family" of states suggests researchers have found ways to engineer more robust or specialized quantum superpositions that maintain their delicate quantum properties longer or perform better in specific applications. The work represents important progress in quantum state engineering, which is fundamental not just for quantum computing but for the emerging field of quantum sensing that promises to revolutionize precision measurements in physics and beyond. These advances in creating and controlling quantum superpositions could lead to sensors capable of detecting phenomena currently beyond our measurement capabilities.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Quantum mechanics, unlike classical physics, allows objects to exist in more than one state at the same time. This idea is often illustrated by Schrödinger's cat, imagined as being both alive and dead until it is observed. In the laboratory, physicists can create less dramatic but very real versions of this effect by placing atoms, light or motion into two distinct quantum states at once. Creating and controlling these superpositions is essential for applications ranging from quantum computing to precision timekeeping.
