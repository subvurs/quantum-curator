Ooredoo Implements Quantum Key Distribution Link on Qatar’s Core Dark Fiber Infrastructure

Source: Quantum Computing Report June 03, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This deployment marks a significant milestone as Qatar becomes one of the first nations to integrate quantum key distribution directly into its national telecommunications infrastructure, moving QKD beyond laboratory demonstrations into real-world operational use. The collaboration between Ooredoo, academia, and the Ministry of Defense reflects the growing recognition among governments that quantum-safe communications are becoming a national security imperative as quantum computers threaten current encryption standards. What's particularly noteworthy is the integration into existing dark fiber infrastructure, demonstrating how QKD can be practically deployed using current telecom assets rather than requiring entirely new networks. This implementation could serve as a blueprint for other nations looking to quantum-secure their critical communications infrastructure before large-scale quantum computers emerge.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Ooredoo Qatar, in a joint national initiative with Hamad Bin Khalifa University (HBKU) and the Ministry of Defense, has completed the deployment of Qatar’s first quantum-safe communications link. This infrastructure project establishes a fully operational, end-to-end Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) framework integrated directly into a live telecommunications environment.
