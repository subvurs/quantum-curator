Microsoft Moves Up Quantum-Safe Security Deadline to 2029

Source: The Quantum Insider July 01, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

AI Commentary

This article shows Microsoft accelerating its quantum‑safe encryption rollout to 2029, signalling that industry now treats post‑quantum cryptography as an imminent engineering challenge rather than a speculative future threat. The move aligns with recent breakthroughs in fault‑tolerant qubit designs and the ongoing NIST standardisation of lattice‑based and code‑based schemes, which together are compressing the timeline for deploying new crypto primitives. For enterprises, the earlier deadline means they must begin migration planning now to avoid costly retrofits later, while also underscoring that practical quantum computers capable of breaking RSA are still years away. Nonetheless, the push highlights a growing consensus that early preparedness will be a competitive advantage in the emerging quantum era.
