Unlocking scalable entanglement will enable next-generation quantum computing

Source: Phys.org Quantum March 26, 2026 Curated by Mark Eatherly

Curator's Take

This article highlights one of quantum computing's most fundamental challenges: creating and maintaining entanglement at scale, which is essential for quantum computers to achieve their promised computational advantages over classical systems. While the summary presents familiar quantum computing benefits, the focus on scalable entanglement addresses a critical bottleneck that has limited current quantum computers to small problem sizes and specific use cases. Successfully scaling entanglement across hundreds or thousands of qubits would mark a pivotal transition from today's noisy intermediate-scale quantum devices to fault-tolerant quantum computers capable of solving real-world problems. This represents a key engineering frontier where breakthroughs could dramatically accelerate the timeline for practical quantum advantage across multiple industries.

— Mark Eatherly

Summary

Quantum computing promises to transform our world in rapid, radical and revolutionary ways: solving in seconds problems that would take classical computers years, accelerating the discovery of new medicines, creating sustainable materials, optimizing complex systems, and strengthening cybersecurity. It does so using qubits, the quantum counterparts of classical bits, which can occupy multiple states simultaneously and enable a fundamentally new kind of computation.
