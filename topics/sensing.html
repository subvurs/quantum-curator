Quantum-Informed Portfolio Selection: An End-to-End Pipeline Validated on Trapped-Ion Hardware with Real Market Data

Portfolio diversification - a cornerstone of modern investment management - can be formulated as a Maximum Independent Set (MIS) problem on asset correlation graphs. Solving this problem at scale is computationally challenging, motivating the exploration of quantum algorithms for practical financial...

This article demonstrates the first end‑to‑end quantum‑enhanced workflow for a real‑world finance problem, showing that a hybrid QAOA‑driven reduction (qReduMIS) can turn noisy trapped‑ion hardware into a useful subroutine for maximum‑independent‑set portfolio selection on graphs as large as 225 assets. By using shallow QAOA circuits to “freeze” promising vertices and then handing the reduced problem to provably optimal classical solvers, the authors achieve approximation ratios above 0.96 and markedly higher success probabilities than raw QAOA alone—an encouraging sign that near‑term quantum devices can add concrete value in high‑dimensional optimization. The work ties directly into the broader push for practical hybrid algorithms on NISQ hardware, while also reminding readers that scaling to larger portfolios will still require improvements in gate fidelity and error mitigation before quantum advantage becomes routine.
