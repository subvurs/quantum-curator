Joined-up thinking in quantum metrology: why collaboration is the secret of success

NPL has joined forces with other leading National Metrology Institutes to shape the international standards effort in quantum technologies The post Joined-up thinking in quantum metrology: why collaboration is the secret of success appeared first on Physics World .

This article highlights a crucial but often overlooked aspect of quantum technology development: the need for standardized measurement protocols that will enable global interoperability. As quantum sensors achieve unprecedented precision in measuring everything from gravitational waves to magnetic fields, establishing common international standards becomes essential for comparing results across different labs and transitioning these technologies from research to commercial applications. The collaboration between National Metrology Institutes like NPL represents a foundational step toward ensuring that quantum sensing breakthroughs can be validated, reproduced, and ultimately trusted by industries ranging from healthcare to navigation. While quantum sensing often gets less attention than quantum computing, this standards work could prove equally important for bringing practical quantum advantages to everyday technologies.
