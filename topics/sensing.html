Joined-up thinking in quantum metrology: why collaboration is the secret of success

NPL has joined forces with other leading National Metrology Institutes to shape the international standards effort in quantum technologies The post Joined-up thinking in quantum metrology: why collaboration is the secret of success appeared first on Physics World .

This collaboration between the UK's National Physical Laboratory and other national metrology institutes represents a crucial step toward establishing unified quantum sensing standards that will be essential as quantum technologies transition from lab curiosities to commercial realities. The effort addresses a critical infrastructure need - without internationally agreed-upon measurement standards, quantum sensors measuring everything from magnetic fields to gravitational waves risk becoming incompatible islands of innovation. This coordinated approach mirrors how classical metrology standards enabled global trade and scientific collaboration, suggesting that quantum metrology could soon provide the measurement backbone for next-generation navigation systems, medical diagnostics, and fundamental physics research. The timing is particularly significant as quantum sensing applications are among the nearest-term quantum technologies poised for widespread deployment.
