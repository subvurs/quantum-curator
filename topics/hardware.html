Single metasurface could generate record numbers of trapped neutral atoms

Technique boosts prospects for building quantum computers with more than 100,000 qubits The post Single metasurface could generate record numbers of trapped neutral atoms appeared first on Physics World .

This breakthrough in metasurface technology represents a major leap toward massively scalable quantum computers using neutral atoms, potentially enabling systems with over 100,000 qubits - far beyond today's largest quantum processors. The ability to trap record numbers of neutral atoms using a single metasurface could dramatically simplify the hardware architecture needed for large-scale quantum computing, addressing one of the field's most pressing challenges around scaling up qubit counts while maintaining control precision. Neutral atom platforms have emerged as strong contenders for fault-tolerant quantum computing due to their inherent identical properties and flexible connectivity, making this advance particularly significant for the race toward practical quantum advantage. This development positions neutral atom quantum computers as increasingly viable alternatives to superconducting and trapped ion systems, especially for applications requiring massive parallelism.
