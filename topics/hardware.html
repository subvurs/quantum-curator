Super-Constant Weight Dicke States in Constant Depth Without Fanout

An $n$-qubit Dicke state of weight $k$, is the uniform superposition over all $n$-bit strings of Hamming weight $k$. Dicke states are an entanglement resource with important practical applications in the NISQ era and, for instance, play a central role in Decoded Quantum Interferometry (DQI). Further...

This research delivers a significant breakthrough in quantum circuit design by showing how to prepare important quantum states called Dicke states using remarkably shallow circuits without requiring expensive fan-out operations. Dicke states serve as crucial building blocks for quantum algorithms and appear prominently in applications like Decoded Quantum Interferometry, but previous methods for creating these states with large Hamming weights demanded deep circuits or powerful fan-out gates that are challenging to implement on real quantum hardware. The work establishes tight theoretical boundaries for what's possible with constant-depth quantum circuits, proving that certain Dicke states can be prepared efficiently while others fundamentally cannot. Most practically, this advances our understanding of how to leverage the limited circuit depth available on today's noisy quantum devices, potentially enabling more sophisticated quantum algorithms within current hardware constraints.
