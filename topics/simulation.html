Quanscient and Haiqu Demonstrate 15-Step Nonlinear Fluid Simulation on IBM Quantum Hardware

Insider Brief PRESS RELEASE — Researchers from Quanscient, a leader in cloud-based multiphysics simulation technology and quantum algorithms, and Haiqu, a leading developer of quantum middleware, today announced a new algorithm that can significantly advance the use of quantum computing in real-worl...

This breakthrough represents a significant step toward practical quantum computational fluid dynamics, as simulating nonlinear fluid flows around obstacles is a cornerstone challenge in engineering applications from aerospace design to climate modeling. The successful execution of a 15-step simulation on current IBM quantum hardware suggests that quantum algorithms for complex fluid dynamics are becoming more viable despite the limitations of today's noisy intermediate-scale quantum devices. The collaboration between Quanscient's simulation expertise and Haiqu's middleware optimization highlights how specialized quantum software stacks are becoming crucial for translating theoretical quantum advantages into real engineering workflows. While still early-stage, this work points toward quantum computing eventually tackling the computationally intensive fluid simulations that currently require massive supercomputing resources.
