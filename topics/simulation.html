Yuval Boger interviews Helmut Katzgraber, general partner at 55North and former quantum leader at AWS. They discuss Helmut’s view that the most promising near- to mid-term quantum applications are in quantum simulation for materials, chemicals, and pharma, rather than optimization. This interview spotlights a growing consensus that quantum simulation—especially for materials science, chemistry and drug discovery—is likely to deliver the first commercially viable quantum advantage, shifting focus away from the often‑hyped optimization use cases. Helmut Katzgraber’s perspective also underscores Europe’s strategic push to build a full‑stack ecosystem, from venture funding to cloud and on‑prem hardware, which could accelerate talent and startup formation across the continent. For readers, the discussion signals where investment dollars and research effort may be most fruitfully directed in the next few years, while reminding them that practical breakthroughs will still depend on scaling error‑corrected qubits and robust software tools.
