Credit: Google Quantum AI whitepaper. By Mohamed Abdel-Kareem Recent technical disclosures from Google Quantum AI and the ...

This article tackles one of quantum computing's most pressing questions: when will quantum computers actually threaten current cryptographic systems like those protecting blockchain networks? Recent estimates from Google Quantum AI and Oratomic are refining our understanding of the quantum resources needed to break 256-bit elliptic curve cryptography, which secures Bitcoin and many other cryptocurrencies. These updated calculations are crucial for determining realistic timelines for quantum advantage in cryptography and help inform when organizations need to transition to quantum-resistant security protocols. The work represents a more nuanced view of the quantum threat, moving beyond theoretical possibilities to practical resource requirements that consider real-world constraints like error rates and logical qubit overhead.
