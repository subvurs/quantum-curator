The work is particularly significant because it bridges pure theory with practical implementation, demonstrating their "QSIGS" algorithm on actual trapped-ion hardware to distinguish spectral stability regions around exceptional points. This represents a rare example of moving from fundamental complexity theory to working quantum algorithms that could help predict and control the behavior of open quantum systems and non-Hermitian materials.
