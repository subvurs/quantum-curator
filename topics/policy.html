Chipmunq: Fault-Tolerant Compiler for Chiplet Quantum Architectures

As quantum computing advances toward fault-tolerance through quantum error correction, modular chiplet architectures have emerged to provide the massive qubit counts required while overcoming fabricat...

This article addresses a critical bottleneck in scaling quantum computers: how to efficiently compile fault-tolerant quantum circuits for the modular chiplet architectures that will be essential for reaching millions of qubits. Chipmunq's breakthrough lies in understanding that traditional compilation approaches fall apart when dealing with quantum error correction codes spread across multiple chiplets connected by sparse, noisy links. The compiler's dramatic improvements—86% reduction in circuit depth and two orders of magnitude better logical error rates—could be the difference between theoretical fault-tolerance and practical quantum advantage. As companies like IBM and Google push toward modular quantum systems, tools like Chipmunq will be essential infrastructure for making these architectures actually work in practice.
