How Quantum Circuits Actually Learn: A Causal Identification of Genuine Quantum Contributions

Attributing performance gains in quantum machine learning to genuine quantum resources rather than to classical architectural scaling remains an open methodological challenge. We address this by intro...

This research tackles one of quantum machine learning's most pressing questions: are performance improvements actually coming from quantum effects, or just from having more parameters and clever circuit design? Using sophisticated causal analysis across multiple circuit types and datasets, the authors found that genuine quantum contributions (like entanglement) account for less than 1% of performance gains, while classical architectural factors dominate by a ratio of 13:1. While this might sound disappointing, it's actually valuable news—it means current quantum ML algorithms are operating far below their quantum potential and provides a clear roadmap for designing circuits that genuinely leverage quantum resources rather than just mimicking classical neural networks with quantum hardware.
