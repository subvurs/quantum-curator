Quantum physicists Charles Bennett and Gilles Brassard win $1m Turing Award

Duo bag award often described as the “Nobel Prize in Computing” The post Quantum physicists Charles Bennett and Gilles Brassard win $1m Turing Award appeared first on Physics World .

This recognition of Charles Bennett and Gilles Brassard with computing's most prestigious award underscores how foundational their quantum cryptography work has become to both quantum physics and computer science. The duo's pioneering BB84 protocol from 1984 essentially launched the field of quantum cryptography by demonstrating how quantum mechanics could guarantee absolutely secure communication—a breakthrough that seemed purely theoretical at the time but now underpins commercial quantum key distribution systems protecting sensitive data worldwide. Their win marks a significant moment where the quantum computing revolution is being acknowledged at the highest levels of the computing establishment, validating decades of research that bridges fundamental physics with practical information security. This award also highlights how quantum technologies are increasingly viewed not as exotic physics experiments but as transformative computing paradigms worthy of the same recognition given to classical computing pioneers.
