Vine Codes: Low-Overhead Quantum LDPC Codes on a Planar Square Grid

The surface code is a promising route towards large-scale quantum computing, requiring only nearest-neighbour gates amenable to superconducting hardware. However, surface codes incur large qubit overheads. Novel quantum low-density parity check (qLDPC) codes promise to reduce overheads but require l...

This article shows that truly low‑overhead quantum LDPC codes can be realized on the same planar superconducting chips that currently host surface‑code experiments, eliminating the need for long‑range couplers that have hampered qLDPC adoption. By introducing “Vine Codes” with open boundaries and routing qubits, the authors demonstrate up to a 28 % reduction in data‑qubit count at distance 7 and an overall ~18 % saving even when routing overhead is included, while still outperforming the surface code in realistic noise simulations at $10^{-3}$ error rates. If these scaling advantages persist at larger distances, Vine Codes could accelerate the transition from prototype devices to fault‑tolerant processors without redesigning existing hardware architectures.
