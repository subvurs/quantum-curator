Intrinsic locality dimension of quantum codes

Quantum error-correcting codes are a cornerstone of quantum computing, with broad and profound connections to physics and mathematics. In this work, we introduce the notion of intrinsic locality dimension of stabilizer codes that is independent of any background geometry and naturally incorporates f...

This article introduces a powerful new mathematical framework for understanding quantum error-correcting codes by defining their "intrinsic locality dimension" - a measure that captures how codes behave geometrically without being tied to specific physical layouts. The breakthrough extends fundamental theoretical limits on quantum codes beyond traditional topological codes to include promising new families like bivariate bicycle codes, which are generating excitement for their potential in near-term quantum computers. By unifying different types of codes under one mathematical umbrella, this work provides theorists and engineers with new tools to understand the fundamental trade-offs between error protection, gate operations, and hardware constraints. The research also delivers sobering news for self-correcting quantum memories, effectively ruling out certain approaches in three-dimensional systems that had been hoped to naturally resist errors without active correction.
