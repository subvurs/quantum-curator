Tsim: Fast Universal Simulator for Quantum Error Correction

We present Tsim, an open-source high-throughput simulator for universal noisy quantum circuits targeting quantum error correction. Tsim represents quantum circuits as ZX diagrams, where Pauli channels are modeled as parameterized vertices. Diagrams are simplified via parameterized ZX rules, and then...

This open-source simulator addresses a critical bottleneck in quantum error correction research by dramatically accelerating the simulation of noisy quantum circuits through clever ZX diagram representations and GPU acceleration. The key breakthrough is achieving linear scaling with Clifford gates while only paying exponential costs for non-Clifford operations, making it possible to efficiently simulate the low-magic circuits typical in quantum error correction protocols. By maintaining compatibility with the widely-used Stim framework while adding support for T gates and arbitrary rotations, Tsim fills an important gap in the quantum computing simulation ecosystem and could significantly speed up the development of fault-tolerant quantum algorithms.
